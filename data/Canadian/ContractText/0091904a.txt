ARTICLE I
PURPOSE OF AGREEMENT
1.01	The parties agree that the general purpose of this Agreement is to promote the mutual interests of the Company and its employees and to provide for fair and equitable earnings, labour standards, hours of work, working conditions, to protect the safety and health of all employees and to provide for prompt and equitable disposition of grievances or disputes which may arise between the parties.
1.02	Should any part of this Agreement be rendered or declared illegal by reason cf any existing or subsequently enacted legislation or by any decree cf a court of competent jurisdiction or by decision of any authorized government agency, such invalidation cf such part or provisions shall not invalidatethe remainder thereof.
1.03	Both parties may mutually agree to amend or supplement this Agreement at any time.
ARTICLE II
MANAGEMENT RIGHTS
2.01	The Union recognizes that the Company has the exclusive control of matters relating to the management and conduct cf its business, the planning, processing and determining the methods o| plant operation and the kind and use of equipment and materials.
2.02	Except as otherwise provided by this Agreement, the Company has the exclusive right of selection and direction of the working force including the size and composition thereof, the right to hire, promote, transfer, lay-off,to discipline and discharge, with just cause, and to establish and maintain rules and regulations covering the operation and conduct of employees.
2.03	Subject to the expressed provisions of this Agreement, these statements of management functions shall not be deemed to exclude other functions not specifically stated herein and any of the rights, powers or authorities which management has relating to management functions are retainedexclusively by the Company. The Company shall not exercise the right to direct the workforce in a discriminatory, unequitable, or unfair manner.

The Company recognizes the Union as the sole and exclusive bargaining agentfor all employees of Levi Strauss & Co. (Canada) Inc., in all facilities inthe City of Edmonton and the Provinceof Alberta, with the exception of clerical or office personnel, supervisory personnel, instructors and persons ranked above these classifications.

All references in this Agreement to the employee or feminine gender shall be considered to be both masculine and feminine in scope.
Inthis Agreement, words using the singular include the plural and the plural singular where the text so indicates.
The Company shall not hereinafter enter into any agreement with any individual employeeto guarantee any length cf employment.
Employeesshall not be discriminatedagainst or dismissed because of activities in the interest of the Union.

ARTICLE V
UNION SECURITY
5.01	It is agreed that all employees shall become members of the Union and remain members in good standing upon completion of a probationary period of thirty (30) working days of continuous service with the Company, except inthe caseofthose employees who enter the Official Training Program of the Company who shall become members cf the Union not laterthan sixty (60) days after the date of commencement cf their employment.
5.02	The Company agrees that they will deduct from the earnings cf the employees who have completed their probationary period, Union Initiation Fees, as well as appropriate Monthly Union Dues and Fines, upon written authorization from the employee concerned, and will remit any money so deducted to the Union. A receipt from the Union for all amounts so deducted shall be considered adischarge to the Company.
5.03	Short-term employees, or part-time employees working no more than 20 hours a week shall become temporary members of the Union after 30 days of employment. It is understood that the duration of employmentfor short-term and part-time employees shall be no longer than three months, after which they shall become full Union members. Short-term or part-time employees shall not be hired unless present employees have full-time work.
5.04	The Company shall supply the Union Business Representative with a seniority list of all departments every three (3) months. Any error with respectto an employee’s positionon the seniority list shall be corrected promptly. The Company will not be held liable for actions taken on the basis of the seniority list prior to the error having been brought to the Company’s attention.

ARTICLE IX LAY-OFF
Where lay-off becomes necessary, it shall be put into effect on an operation basis within the line affected. The employees with the least seniority with the Company, who are working on the operation affected, shall be the first to be laid off.
The Company shall not employ temporary workers while any full-time employees are laid off.
In the event of a temporary reduction of labour force, the Company agrees to, if at all possible, furnish all available work during a continuous period before laying off employees, except upon mutual agreement between the parties in cases where it might be in the interest of the employees affected to work on a part-time basis.

ARTICLE XIV
LUNCH PERIOD/REST PERIOD
The Company and the Union agree that lunch periods and rest periods are essential to the health and welfare cf employees, therefore, employees must stop working during these periods of time.
A lunch period cf one-half hour (unpaid) shall be provided for all employees who are working a regular eight (8) hour day.
Two (2) fifteen (15) minute rest periods shall be provided each day.
A lunch period of twenty minutes (unpaid) shall be provided for all employeeswho work the regular night shift.
One (1) fifteen (15) minute rest period shall be provided for all night shift employees.
Anyone working a regular night shift cf five (5) hours or less is entitled to one (1) fifteen (15) minute break period every two (2) hours.
During the first two (2) hours cf overtime, employees are entitled to one (1) fifteen (15) minute break, to betaken at the end of the regular eight (8) hour shift.
If the employees work three (3) to four (4) hours overtime, they are entitled to two (2) fifteen (15) minute rest periods. One is to be observed at the end c£ the regular eight (8) hour shift, the other to be taken mid-way through the overtime.

For the purpose cf this Agreement, a grievance is defined as any difference between the employer and the employee, or a group of employees or a policy grievance between the employer or the Union concerning the interpretation, application, administration or alleged violation of specific terms of the Agreement. If an employee feels that he or she has a grievance, it shall be reported in the first instance to the immediate supervisor of such an employee as soon as possible. No further steps shall be taken and the grievance shall not be put into effect until the immediatesupervisor has first been given an opportunity to adjust the grievance. In presenting such a grievance to the immediate supervisor, an employee may be assisted by the Union Business Representative or by the Shop Steward.
I such grievance cannot be settled to the satisfaction of the employee(s) concernedwithin two (2) working days after the grievance has been reported to the immediate supervisor, or within any longer period of time that may be mutually agreeable upon at that time, then the following steps of grievance procedure shall be followed in due order.
Step 1
All grievances shall be stated in writing on the Standard Grievanceform and signed by the grieving employee(s) setting cut the nature cf the complaintin shortterms, the date when the grievance first occurred and the section(s) of the collective agreement allegedly violated, and shall contain the relief requested by the employee.
