Article 6 - GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE:
6.01	A grievance is a difference arising between an employee and/or the Union
and the Company, as to the meaning or application o f any provision of the Agreement. Unless agreed to by both the Company and the Union, no grievance sh ill be processed, should the alleged circumstances have originated or occurred more than thirty (30) calendar days prior to its original presentation in writing. No grievance shall be processed by the Union on behalf of an employee without the employee being notified by the Union of such proceeding.
5
6.02	If a complaint or question should arise, which is not settled to the satisfaction of the employee or employees concerned, after discussion with the immediate foreman concerned, or the Superintendent in the absence of the foreman. Viftin one (1) working day, or within any such longer period as may be mutually agreed upon the time, then the Mowing steps of the grievance procedure shall be invoked, in order:
6.03	Step#l:
The Production Manager or his designate may confer with not less than three (3) representatives of the Grievance Committee of the employees and settle the grievance, subject to the confirmation of the management of the Company, and if the grievance is not settled within three (3) working days to the satisfaction of the employees, representatives may appeal to the General Manager ofthe Company, but the employees must carry out the orders ofthe Produdion Manager or his designate, pending the appeal.
6.04	Sten #2:
If the grievance is not then settled, the Union shall state the grievance in writing and deliver a copy to the General Manager, or his designate requesting a meeting which will be held within two (2) working days or such longer time as may be mutually agreed upon. At this meeting, the Business Agent of the Union may be present if his presence is requested by either party.
6.05	No Company official shall take up a grievance personally with any affected employee without a Shop Steward being present once the grievance has been formally lodged, in writing, by the Grievance Committee.
6.06	Step #3:
If the grievance is not settled at the meeting held under Step #2, the grievance shall be discussed within a further period offive (5) working days, between the General Manager ofthe Coirpany or his designate and a representative ofthe Union, who shall meet and attempt to reach a settlement.
6.07	Any arrangement or decision agreed to underthe foregoing steps shall be confirmed and set forth in writing, and be binding upon the Union, the Ccrrpany,and the employee or employees concerned.

for time lost from work during regular working hours while processing and discussing grievances of employees with representatives of the Company. Meetings to discuss grievances shall be held during regular office hours of the Company.
Article 7 - ARBITRATION:
7.01	Should the parties fail to reach an agreement under the foregoing procedure, either party may refer the matter to arbitration. In such went, the requesting patty shall, within (1Q) working days of completion of the steps referred to above, serve waiisi notice on the other party by registered mail of its request, stating the matter to be arbitrated.
7.02	The requesting party shall then notify the other party, in writing, within five (5) working days, of its serving such notice, of the name of the person to represent it as an arbitrator, and the receiving party shall, within five (5) working days of receipt of such name, notify the requesting party of the name of its arbitrator. The two arbitrators so named shall then meet and attempt to settle the dispute, and failing agreement, they shall, within five (5) voddrg days after their last meeting, nans an impartial representative to assist them in settling the dispute. The two persons named and the impartial representative, shall form a Board of Arbitration, herein Galled "the Board", and the impartial representative shall act as Chairman of the Board. Should the two arbitrators fail to agree on the choice of the impartial representative, the Minister of Labour for the Province of Manitoba shall be asked to select such person. The majority decision of the Board shall be final and binding upon both parties. By mutual agreement, the parties may refer the grievance to a single arbitrator.
7.03	The Board of Arbitration shall havejurisdiction and authority only to interpret, apply or determine compliance with the provisions of this Agreement insofar as shall be necessary to the determination of grievances appealed to the Board, but the Board shall not havejurisdiction or authority to add to, deduct from, or alter in any way, the provisions of this Agreement.
7.04	The Board cf Arbitration shall adopt its own procedure for determining the matter in dispute.
7.05	No person shall be appointed as an Arbitratorwho has been involved in any attempt to settle the grievance. The cost of the arbitrators appointed by the Company and the Union shall be borne by each party respectively, and the cost o ft he third merrber of the Board shall be borne equally by the Company arid the Union.

SENIORITY:
Employees shall be considered on probation and shall not be entitled to seniority rig hts until they have completed forty-five (45) working days' service with the Company. Upon completion of the probationary period, seniority shall be dated from the date of employment During an employee's probationary period, he shall be considered as being employed on a trial basis and may be discharged or laid off at the discretion of the Company arid neither such employee nor the Union shall have recourse to grievance or arbitration procedures. Employees who restrict their availability shall not accumulate seniority. This shall not prevent seniority already accrued from being used in recall from lay-off
As of the first week of May and the first week of October of each year, the Carpany shall prepare and maintain seniority lists; such lists shall be posted on the Company bulletin boards or will otherwise be made available for the employees to examine on request. A copy of all seniority lists will be mailed to the Union office. The classification of employees as of the date o f preparation of seniority lists will be shown on the said seniority lists.
Promotions, transfers, demotions, upgrading, layoff and recalls from layoffs will normally be based upon seniority, provided there is equality of skill, ability and physical fitness amongst the eligible employees.
All employees may exercise their seniority rights and shall be entitled to a trial period of up to thirty (30) working days in layoffs, recalls from layoff, transfers, reduction in staff far any reason, or when making applications fir vacancies posted on the bulletin board. The rate of pay applicable during such trial period shall be that of the rate the employee was earning prior fo die trial period commencing, except that where the classification rate at which the employee is working during the trial period is less, then the lesser rate shall apply, where a trial period is applicable, training will be provided.
Where it is found necessary to generally reduce die working force, seniority of all plant employees will be the guiding factor, so long as it does not prevent the Company from maintaining a workable farce of employees who are qualified and willing to do the work which is available.
