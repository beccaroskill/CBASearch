Article 1 - SCOPE AND EXTENT:
1.01	This Agreement applies to all plant employees (hereincalled the
Plant Employees) of the Company, in the city of Winnipeg, Manitoba, as designated by Certificate #3 52 of the Manitoba Labour Board, dated February 6,1952, but does not extend to employees employed as office staff: or to Company officials who act in a confidential or supervisory capacity. The company shall, upon the written request of the Union, send to the Union a list of Company officials so acting, and will indicate by appropriate job titles, the nature and extent of their authority.
Article 2 - RECOGNITION:
2.01	The Plant Employees have selected the Union as their sole and exclusive bargaining agent, and the Company recognizes the Union as such.
2.02	The Company shall recognize the Business Agent of the Union as a representative of the Plant Employees, and shall extend to him every privilege as such.

Article 3 - RELATIONSHIP
3.01	All Plant Employees at present employed, who are new members of the Union, and all plant employees hereinafter employed, shall, from the date of this Agreement, or within thirty (30) calendar days of employment, become and remain members in good standing of the Union, for the life of this Agreement.
3.02	The Company agrees that all new employees shall, on the first day of employment,, sign a post-dated application for membership in the Union, effective thirty (30) calendar days later, which shall provide for Company deduction of Union initiation fees, dues and assessments. For the convenience of the Company, application forms shall be supplied by the secretary of the Union.
3.03	The Company shall deduct from the pay of each plant employee who so requests the Company, in writing, t o do so, all Union initiation fees, dues and assessments uniformly applied.
3.04	All such deductions for Union fees, dues and assessments shall be taken from the first pay period of the month,
3.05	All sums deducted, together with a record of those from whem such deductions have been made, and the amount, shall be forwarded to the Secretary Treasurer of the Union not later than the twentieth (20th) day of the calendar month in which such deductions are made.
3.06	The Company shall supply the Union, at least once per month, with the names of all new employees and those having left the service of the Company.
3.07	When new help shall be required, the Company shall notify the Union of its requirements and the Union shall endeavour to send competent personnel to the Company, but the Company shall not be obliged to engage anyone so sent by the Union.
3.08	The terms and conditions as negotiated by the parties of this Agreement shall apply to all plant employees and no alteration of wages, increase in hours of labour, or changes in working conditions shall be made to any plant employees without prior discussion with the Union or its representatives.
The Company will permit the Chief Steward of the Plant to enter any department to conduct business on behalf of the Union, provided he first notifies the department head and further provided he does not unduly take up the time of, employees at work.

Article 6 - GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE:
6.01	A grievance is a difference arising between an employee and/or the Union and the Company, as to the meaning or application of any provision of the Agreement, Unless agreed to by both the Company and the Union, no grievance shall be processed, should the alleged circumstances have originated or occurred more than thirty (30) calendar days prior to its original presentation in writing. No grievance shall be processed by the Union on behalf of an employee without the employee being notified by the Union of such proceeding.
6.02	If a complaint or question should arise, which is not settled to the satisfaction of the employee or employees concerned, after discussionwith the immediate foreman concerned, or the
Superintendent in the absence of the foranan." within one Cl) working day, or within any such longer period as may be mutually agreed upon the time, then the following steps of the grievance procedure shall be invoked, in order:
6.03	Step #l!
The Production Manager or his designatemay confer with not less than three (3) representatives of the Grievance Committee of the employees and settle the grievance, subject to the confirmation of the management of the Company, and if the grievance is not settled within three (3) working days to the satisfaction of the employees, representatives may appeal to the General Manager of the Company, but the employees must carry out the orders of the Production Manager or his designate, pending the appeal.

7.01	. Should the parties fail to reach an agreement under the

^•02	The requesting party shall then notify the other party, in
writing, within five (5) working days, of its serving such notice, of the name of the person to represent it as an arbitrator, and the receiving party shall, within five (5) working days of receipt of such name, notify the requesting party of the name of its arbitrator. The majority decision of the Board shall be final and binding upon both parties.

7.05	No person shall be appointed as an Arbitrator who has been involved in any attempt to settle the grievance. The cost of the arbitrators appointed by the Company and the Union shall be borne by each party respectively, and the cost of the third member of the Board shall be borne equally by the Company and the Union.

Such special grievances may be settledby confirming the Management's action in dismissing the employee, or by reinstatement of the employee with full compensation f o r t i m e lost, or by any other arrangement which, in the opinion of the parties, is fair and reasonable.

Article 10 - CONTINUITY OF OPERATIONS:
10.01	There shall be no strike, slow-down or stoppage of work on the part of the Union, or any employee, and there shallbe no lockout by the Company during the life of this Agreement, or during the period stipulated by Manitoba Law, for the negotiation of a new Agreement.
10.02	A shut-down occasioned by any cause beyond control of the Company, or by any changes in method of operation, shall not constitute a lockout.
10.03	Any employee who contravenes the provisions of thi s Article may be subject to discharge by the Company and, if discharged, may have recourse to the grievance procedure,
Article 11 - SENIORITY:
11.01	Employees shall be considered on probation and shall not be entitled to seniority rights until they have completed forty—five (45) working days' service with the Company, Upon completion of the probationary period, seniority shall be dated from the date of employment. During an employee’s probationary period, he shallbe considered as being employed on a trial basis and may be discharged or laid off at the discretion of the Company and neither such employee nor the Union shall have recourse to grievance or arbitration procedures, Employees who restrict their availability shall not accumulate seniority. This shall not prevent seniority already accrued from being used in recall from lay-off.
11.02	As of the first week of M^ and the first week of October of each year, the Company shall prepare and maintain a seniority list; such list shall be posted on the Company bulletin boards or w il 1 otherwise be made available for the employees to examine on request. A copy of all seniority list will be mailed to the Union office. The classification of employees as of the date of preparation of seniority lists will be shown on the said seniority lists.

Article 11 (Cont1 d.)
11.03	Promotions, transfers, demotions, upgrades, layoff and recalls from layoffs will normally be based upon seniority, provided there is equality of skill, ability and physical fitness amongst the eligible employees.
11.04	All employees may exercise their seniority rights and shall be
entitled to a trial period of up to thirty (30) working days in layoffs, recalls from layoff, transfers, reduction in staff for any reason, or when making applications for vacancies posted on the bulletin board. The rate of pay applicable during such trial period shall be that of the rate the employee was earning prior to the trial period commencing, except that where the classification rate at which the employee is working during the trial period is less, then the lesser rate shall apply, where a trial period is applicable, training will be provided.
11.05	Whae it i s found necessary to generally reduce the working force, seniority of all plant employees will be the guiding factor, so long as it does not prevent the Company from maintaining a workable force of employees who are qualified and willing t o do the work which is available.

An employee who is working at a full-time job when recalled shall first contact the Company as soon as possible and then shall report for work within seven (7) days. However, if the employee is not working at a full-time job, he shall first contact the Company as soon as possible and then shall report to work within three (3) days. Failure to report to work within the time limits set out herein shall mean an employee shall forfeit any claim to recall. Any recalls shall, if made by telephone, be immediately confirmed by registered mail or telegram addressed to the last known address on record with the Company.
11.07	Where the Company is unable to contact an employee by telephone who is eligible for recall following layoff, the Company may call the next eligible employee.
