MANALTA COAL LTD, HIGHVALE MINE, INTHE PROVINCE OF ALBERTA (hereinafter referred to as the "Company”)

PREAMBLE
The purpose of this Agreement shall be to establish, through negotiations. terms and conditions of employment relating to rates of pay, hours of work and other working conditions of employees, and to provide a method for negotiating settlement of disputes, complaints and grievances of employees, and to establish a harmonious relationship between the Company and the Union.
ARTICI E 1 - SCOPE
This Agreement shall apply to all employees of Manalta Coal Ltd., excluding Engineers, Surveyors, Engineering Technicians, Office &Clerical Staff, Assistant Purchasing Agents, Purchasing Agents, Foremen, and those above the rank of Foreman.
1
ARTICLE 2 - RECOGNITION AND MANAGEMENT RIGHTS
1) Union Recognition
The Company recognizes the Union as the sole collective bargaining agency for the employees covered by this Agreement and hereby consents and agrees to negotiate with the Union, or its designated bargaining representatives, in all matters relating to rates of pay, hours of work, working conditions, disputes, complaints, and grievances of employees. The Company also agrees that the Union may have the assistance of a representative of the United Steelworkers of America in any or all negotiations or discussions between the parties to this Agreement.
2)	Management Rights
The right to hire, retire, promote, demote, discharge or discipline for just cause, and to maintain discipline and efficiency of employees is the exclusive function of the Company, subject to the terms and conditions of the Agreement. All matters concerning the operation of the Company's business not specifically dealt with herein shall be reserved to the Company and be its exclusive responsibility.
Without restricting the generality of the foregoing, the Union further recognizes the jurisdiction of the Company over all operations, buildings, machinery, tools, and employees and the right of the Company to operate and manage its business in all respects in accordance with its commitments and responsibilities.
2
ARTICLE 3 - UNION SECURITY
Every employee who is now or hereafter becomes a member of the Union shall maintain his membership in the Union as a condition of his employment, and every new employee whoso employment commences hereafter shall, within three days after the commencement of his employment, apply for and maintain membership in the Union as a condition of his employment.
The services of an employee shall be immediately terminated upon the request of the Union, should such employee fail to apply for the Union membership as required above, and/or should he fail to maintain his membership in the Union in any manner and/or should he withdraw from the Union.
ARTICI F 4 - CHECK OFF
The Company agrees that upon written request by the Union, accompanied by signed authorization cards, all Union initiation fees, monthly dues, assessments or levies, shall be deducted for, and on behalf of all employees who are members of the Union, and such monies shall be made payable, for deposit only, in the Union Bank Account, and forwarded to the Secretary-Treasurer of the Union not later than the 20th day of each month accompanied by a list of names of all employees for, and on behalf of whom such deductions have been made. Monthly statements showing the names of all additions and deletions of staff shall be forwarded to the Secretary-Treasurer of the Union.
In the event of a contractor or subcontractor being employed by the Company at the mine site or in the event of employees employed by contractors or sub-contractors being directly or indirectly paid by, or under the control and direction of the Company on the mine site in excess of 24 man hours (excluding time spent on warranty work) in any calendar month, the Company will pay to the Union an amount
3
equivalent to one month's union dues for each individual on the
mine site in excess of 24 hours. A statement will be included
with the remittance.

1.	The Company agrees that leave of absence without pay but with maintenance arid accumulation of seniority shall be granted to any designated employee for the conducting of Union business at large.

2.	The Company agrees that a leave of absence without pay to a maximum of one year shall be granted to employees for good and sufficient reason. Such request for a leave of absence will be in writing and the response will also be in writing and no such leave will affect any employee's seniority rights when used for the purpose granted. During a leave of absence, including a military leave, of 30 calendar days or less all benefits will be maintained. Seniority will be maintained and accumulated on leaves of 30 calendar days or less, and maintained for that portion of a leave that is in excess of 30 calendar days. If an employee works elsewhere while on leave of absence, he will lose all seniority unless he has written permission from the Company to do so. Such leave shall be granted without discrimination and in cases of leaves for periods in excess of three months, approval must be obtained from the Company and the Union.

Immediate family shall mean an employee's mother, father, brothers, sisters, spouse, children, mother-in-law and father-in-law. Such leave shall not exceed three days.

ARTICLE 7 - GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE
1. All grievances, except as provided for elsewhere in this agreement, and those relating to an employee who lias acquired seniority and has been discharged, shall be dealt with by following Steps 1, 2, and .3 of this procedure. The grievance of a discharged employee as noted above shall be taken up at Step 3 within 10 days (excluding Saturdays, Sundays and holidays) after the day the employee has been terminated from tho Company.
STEP 1 In the event of any grievance arising during the currency of this Agreement, the employee or employees concerned shall within 10 days after the employee knew or ought to have known of the matter giving rise to the grievance, refer the matter in writing signed by the employee or employees, as the case may be, to a Shop Steward who, it the grievance is considered a legitimate one, shall promptly take the matter up with the designated Foreman of the employee or employees. The designated Foreman and another Company representative shall discuss the grievance with tho Shop Steward and another Union representative, (with the employee or employees present or absent at their option), and shall render a decision within three of the griovor’s regularly scheduled working days, (holidays excluded), following notification of the grievance.
STEP 2 In the event that the designated Foreman does not give a decision satisfactory to the Union, or does not render a decision within the period prescribed above, the Grievance Committee, may within four days, refer the written grievance to the Department Head. The Department Head and othei Company representatives shall discuss the grievance with the Grievance
9
Committee and the Shop Steward, (with the employee or employees concerned present or absent at their option), and shall render a decision within four days, (Saturdays, Sundays and holidays being excluded), of being notified of the grievance, provided however, that if circumstances warrant, this time prescribed may be extended by mutual agreement.
STEP 3 In the event that the Department Head does not give a decision satisfactory to the Union or does not render a decision within the time prescribed above, the Grievance Committee may within five days, (excluding Saturdays, Sundays and holidays), refer the written grievance to the Mine Manager of the Company. The Mine Manager, accompanied by other Company representatives, shall meet with the Grievance Committee and the Shop Steward, (with the employee or employees present or absent at their option), and render his decision within five days, (excluding Saturdays, Sundays and holidays), of referral of the grievance, provided however, the time may be extended by mutual consent.
ARBITRATION
STEP 4 In the event that any grievance or matter in dispute has not been settled through the procedure outlined above, either party may, within fourteen days after the Mine Manager's decision in Step 3, submit the grievance or matter in dispute to arbitration. A single arbitrator shall be used unless either party requests to submit the issue to an Arbitration Board. If, within 10 days after such referral, the parties are unable to agree upon an Arbitrator, the Minister of Labour for the Province of Alberta will be asked to appoint one. Should the issue be referred to a Board of Arbitration,
10
it shall consist of three members, one named by each of the Company and the Union within 10 days of tho referral to arbitration, and tho third, tho Chairman, to be selected by the two previously nominated members within a further 10 clays. In the event of their failure to agree on a Chairman within the time prescribed, they shall notify the Minister of Labour for the Province of Alberta who shall bo asked to name a Chairman. After either the Arbitration Board has been formed, or Arbitrator named, by tho foregoing procedure, the three members, or single Arbitrator, shall meet and hear the evidence of both parties and render a decision within seven clays after the completion of taking evidence.
The decision of either the majority of the Arbitratioti Board or the sole Arbitrator for the matter at issue shall be final and binding on both parties, but the jurisdiction of either the Arbitration Board or of the single Arbitrator shalt be limited to deciding the matter at issue within the existing provisions of the Agreement, and in no event shall either the Arbitration Board or the single Arbitrator have the power to add to, subtract from, alter or amend this Agreement in any respect.
The fees and expenses of the Chairman or single Arbitrator shall be shared equally between the parties. Each party shall pay its own costs and the fees arid expenses of witnesses called by it, arid where applicable, its representative.

ARTICLE 8 - SENIORITY	
1.	The seniority and service of an employee shall be plant-wide and shall be based upon continuous service with the Company and shall date from the time he last
	12
entered the service of the Company, subject to the following:
a)	An employee shall acquire seniority when ho has completed his probationary period of 360 regularly scheduled hours worked, and upon completion of this period, his seniority shall bo retroactive to the date of his hiring.
b)	Continuous service of an employee shall bo broken and seniority shall terminate by reason of any of the following:
j) Dismissal for just and reasonable cause;
ii)	Voluntary resignation,
iii)	Failure to report for work within 10 working days after being notified to report following a layoff, unless the employee can give a reason satisfactory to the Company for his failure to report within the time prescribed;
iv)	Withdrawal of any amount to which that employee is entitled Linder the Company Pension Plans,
v)	A continuous layoff which is equal to one-half of the employee's seniority at the time of layoff, but in no event shall this period exceed two years,
i.e. one year of service - six months.
NOTE: Should an employee break service or lose seniority for any of the foregoing reasons and be subsequently rehired, such employee shall commence employment
13
as a new employee and shall not have any service or seniority from any previous employment with the Company.
c)	Subject to 8(b)(v) an employee's seniority and service shall accumulate during a period of layoff due to lack of work.
2.	Employees shall not be laid off, recalled, promoted or demoted until the Union has been notified of the names of the persons affected and the reasons for such action and a consultation with the Union has taken place in respect to the matter.
In cases of terminations, the Union will be notified, as soon as possible, but not beyond the next working day, excluding Saturday, Sunday, and holidays.
3.	The Company agrees to prepare and post not later than January 31st and July 31st of each year, or at such other time as may be mutually agreed upon, in places easily accessible to all employees, a list of the names of all employees, showing their job classifications, rates of pay and combined seniority standing, the latter being open to protest by employees for 30 days from the date of such submission. Upon proof of error being established by a representative of the Union, correction shall immediately be made. The Company will supply the Union with a copy of seniority lists and corrections thereto, within two working days excluding Saturdays, Sundays and holidays.
