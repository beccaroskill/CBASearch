1.01	COMPANY - UNION RELATIONSHIP
Parties to this Agreement recognize the common dependence of the Company and its employees upon the welfare of the business, aid the importance of maintaining the existing relationship cf harmony aid mutual respect between them.
1.02	LABOUR/MANAGF.MENT MEETINGS
To promote the interest of the Company and to discuss matters of mutual interest which are not proper subjects to be dealt with under the Grievance Procedure, it is agreed to hold Labour/Management .Meetings once monthly. The committee shall consist of three (3) representatives of the Union, the make-up of which will be the President. Local Chairperson aid one other member and three (3) Maiagement representatives selected by Management. Additional representatives may attend a Labour/Management Meeting by mutual agreement between the Company and the Union.
1.03	WORKPLACE RIG UTS
The Company recognizes aid accepts its responsibility to prevent harassment and illegal discrimination it its workplace. as well as its obligations under the Human Rights Act of the Province of Nova Scotia.
The Company and the Union are committed to providing a harassment-free workplace and expect all employees to treat others with courtesy and consideration and to discourage harassment.
Harassment is defined as a "course of vexatious comment or conduct that is known or ought reasonably be known to be unwelcome".
Prevention and reporting of harassment situations is the responsibility of all employees. Any person who believes that an employee has experienced, or is experiencing, workplace harassment, or retaliation for having brought forward a complaint, is encouraged to notify a Workplace Rights Committee Member.
Harassment may take many forms: verbal, physical or visual. It may involve a
7
threat or an implied threat or be perceivedas a condition of employment. The following examples could be considered as harassment but are not meant to cover all potential incidents:
-	Unwelcome remarks, jokes, innuendos, gestures or taunting about a person's body, disability, attire or gender, racial or ethnic backgrounds, colour, place of birth, sexual orientation, citizenship or ancestry;
■	Practicaljokes, pushing, shoving, etc., which cause awkwardness or embarrassment;
" Posting or circulation of offensive photos or visual materials;
■	Refusal to work or converse with an employee because of their racial backgroundor gender, etc;
-	Unwanted physical conduct such as touching, patting, pinching, etc;
• Condescensionor paternalism which undermines self-respect;
■	Backlash or retaliation for the lodging of a complaint or participation in an investigation
The Company and Union agree to maintain the established Workplace Rights Policy and Committee. The Committee will consist of equal number of members from the Company and Union, with there being two (2) co-chairs, one from Management and one from the Union. The Committee will be run by the terms of reference as set out in the policy and by the committee. A list of members and their contact information will be posted and maintained in each department. Employeesare encouraged to seek out a member of the Committee if they feel they have a complaint.
Any harassmentcomplaints shall firstbe addressed by the Workplace Rights Committee. At any point in the complaint procedures, the complainant has the right to make a complaint with the Nova Scotia Human Rights Commission.
Such complaints are investigated by the Nova Scotia Human Rights Commission.
All time limits for filing a grievance shall be suspended until the outcome of the Workplace Rights Committee investigation. At the conclusion of the investigation.the complaint if unresolved may be considered as a grievance for the purposes of the grievance procedure for resolution.
In the event that an employee files a harassment complaint simultaneously with the Human Rights Commission, and the complaint to the Commission is based on the same subject matter as the complaint to the Committee, then the Committee
mill hold its complaint in abeyance pending the outcome of the other forum.
The Workplace Rights Policy will be reviewed with all new employees within the probationary period. The Workplace Rights Committee will make recommendations to Management when refresher training is deemed to be needed.

8.02	GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE
Step 1 An employee having a complaint shoulddiscuss it with his immediate Supervisor, within five (5) employee working days of the event giving rise to the complaint, in order to provide the Supervisor with an opportunity to adjust the complaint. The employee shall be accompanied by his Grievance Committee Member or another Grievance Committee Member if his Member is unavailable. The Supervisor shall reply, in wifing,to the employee's complaint within three (3) employee working days.
Step 2 Failing settlement of the complaint at Step I, a formal written grievance outlining the alleged violation of the Agreement and redress sought shall be presented to the employee's immediate Supervisor within five (5) employee working days of having presented the complaint to the Supervisor. The written grievance must be signed by the employee filing the grievance and must be presented by his Grievance Committee Member. Within five (5) employee working days of the receipt of the grievance, a meeting will be held. The employee'simmediate Supervisor and the Business Unit Manager or their Nominee will meet to discuss the grievance with the employee, and his Grievance Committee Member or anotherCommitteeMemberifhis Member Is unavailable. A written decision to the grievance will be provided within the same five (5) working day time limit. A copy of the decision will be given to the employee, his Grievance Committee Member, and the Committee Local Chairperson. Receiptof the decision by the Committee Local Chairperson, or his designate, shall constitute fulfillmentof the time limits of this Step. If no settlement is reached, then the grievance may proceedto Step 3.
Step 3 Within five (5) employee working days of the receipt of the decision at Step2, the Grievance Committee Local Chairperson, or his designate, msy submitthe grievance to the Human Resources Manager. Step 3 grievanceswill be considered during a meeting that will be held on the third Thursday of the month or on a date mutually agreed upon by the Human Resources Manager and the Grievance Committee Local Chairperson, or his designate. The Step 3 meeting shall include the Human Resources Manager or his nominee, not more than four (4)
17
Management Representatives, the Presidentof the Union, the Grievance Committee Local Chairperson, not more than two (2) members ofthe Grievance Committee, the employee, and the Union Representative, if desired by the Company or the Union. A Step 3 meeting may be cancelled by mutual consent between the Company and the Union. The Human Resources Manager or his nominee shall provide a written decision within five (5) employes working days ofthe Step 3 meeting. A copy of the decision will be given to the employee, his Grievance Committee Member, and the Grievance Committee Local Chairperson. Receipt of the decision by the Grievance Committee Local Chairperson, or his designate, shall constitute fulfilment of the time limits of this Step.
8.03	ARBITRATION NOTIFICATION
If all required steps ofthe grievance procedure have been followed, aid a final settlement ofthe grievance has not been reached at Step No. 3, then the Union may at any time within forty-five (45) calendar days after the decision is given under Step 3, give written notification of its intent to invoke give written notification of fe intent to invoke arbitration, mediation/arbitration, or government mediation as provided inArticle 9. The Union reserves the right to proceed to arbitration on any grievance.
8.04	DISCHARGE AND SUSPENSION GRIEVANCES
If an employee who has completed his probationary period is discharged or suspended withoutjust cause and feels he has been unjustly dealt with, he shall, within seven (7) calendar days after the suspension or discharge, file a written grievance with the Company and the grievance shall be dealt with beginning at Step No. 3 of the grievance procedure.

The Union Representative and the Chairperson ofthe Grievance Committee and'or President of the Local shall meet within five (5) days thereafter with Management to consider the grievance. If final settlement of the grievance is not completed within five (5) days of such
18
meeting, the grievance may be taken to Arbitration as provided in Article 9 at any time within 30 calendar days thereafter,but not later.
8.06	POLICY GRIEVANCES
Any difference arising directly between the Union and the Company as to the interpretationor alleged violation of the provisions of this Agreement may be submitted in writing atStep3 of the grievance procedure.
8.07	TIMELIMITS
Any and all time limits fixed by the grievance procedure and for filing arbitrations may be extended by mutual agreement in writing between the Company and the Union. The matter in dispute shall be deemed to have been abandoned should the Company or the Union not proceed from one step to another within the time limits specified, or as mutually extended as set out herein.
Agrievancewill automatically advance from Step No. 1 to StepNo. 2 of (he Grievance procedure if the Company fails to respond within the time limits specified. or as mutually extended as set out herein.
A grievance will be deemed to have beenjudged valid if the Company fails to respond in StepNo. 3 within the time limits specified, or as mutually extended as

ARTICLE 9 - ARBITRATION
9.0! APPOINTMENT
-In the case of government mediation, the government will appoint a mediator.
In any case in which arbitration or mediation/arbitration shall be required under this Agreement, the Union and the Company shall first attempt to mutual ly agree upon a single Arbitrator within ten (10) days from the date of the receipt of the notice of Arbitration.
Should the Union and the Company fail to agree on the selection of an Arbitrator, then the appointment of an impartial Arbitrator shall be made by the Minister of Labour for the Province of Nova Scotia. The appointee will act as a single Arbitrator.
9.02	LIMITATIONS
The Arbitrator will not be authorized to make any decision which would alter, modify, or amend any part of this Agreement.
However, the Arbitrator may dispose of any discharge or discipline grievance in the manner which he considersjust or equitable.
9.03	DECISION BINDING
The decision of the Arbitrator will be final aid binding upon both parties and the employees concerned, and should, normally, be rendered within 30 calendar days from the time the matter was referred to him. This period may be extended by mutual agreement between the Union and the Company.
9.04	EXPENSES
When the Arbitrator deems it advisable to incur other expenses in connection with the grievance, the Arbitrator will obtain the consent of the parties before incurring such expenses.
Expenses which are incurred in connection with the services of the Arbitrator will be home equally by both parties to this Agreement.
Witness fees and allowances shall be paid by the party calling such witnesses.

This will confirm the agreement reached during 2009 negotiations regarding umbrellajobs.
Using the definition for umbrellajobs as: a lower Group leveljob included in a job description inwhichan employeemust beabletoperformall aspectsofthatjobon a regular basis as stipulated in the job description.
The parties undertake to conduct a joint review of eachjob classificationforthe purpose ofestablishing the umbrellajobs associated with each classification.
A list of jobs that fall under an umbrella will be clearly defined through mutual agreement between the Union and the Company and placed on alljob descriptions.
Any job acquired under an umbrellajob is automatically placed on an employee’s bumping list and as per Article 10.07 (d)(i) an employee will have fourteen days to sign off and return his notification; otherwise any new jobs will become the first possible bumps.
Thejoint review will take place within ninety (90) days following ratification of this agreement.
