SEXUAL HARASSMENT POLICY
Royal Oak Mine Inc...Giant, Northwest Territories Division, is committed to maintain a work environment that is free of sexual harassment. Sexual harassment will not be tolerated and any person who is in breach of this policy will be subject to disciplinary action or discharge.
Royal Oak Mine Inc...Yellowknife Division will not knowingly permit the sexual harassment of any person and will take all reasonable steps to terminate such harassment, as required under the Human Rights Code and/or applicable legislation.
Sexual harassment is any unsolicited and unwelcome sexually oreinted behaviour. It may include but is not limited to the following:
unwanted sexual attention of a persistent or abusive nature, made by a person who knows or ought reasonably to know that such attention is unwanted; or
implied or expressed promise of reward for complying with a sexually oriented request; or
implied or expressed threat of reprisal, in the form cither of actual reprisal or the denial of opportunity, for refusal to comply with a sexually oriented request; or
unwelcome remarks, jokes, innuendos, propositions, or taunting about a person’s body, attire, sex or sexual orientation; or
displaying of pornographic or sexist pictures or materials
An individual who is being subjected to any form of sexual harassment may tell the offender that what is being said or done is offensive and ask that such behaviour stop and/or report the incident, at the earliest opportunity to a supervisor or other management personnel.

This Agreement dated the 20th Day of May, A.D. 1997 between
NATIONAL AUTOMOBILE, AEROSPACE, TRANSPORTATION
AND GENERAL WORKERS UNION OF CANADA (CAW CANADA) AND ITS LOCAL 2304
hereinafter referred to as “The Union”.
OF THE FIRST PART
- and -
ROYAL OAK MINES INC.
GIANT MINE
of the City of Yellowknife, in the Northwest Territories.
hereinafter referred to as “The Company”,
OF THE SECOND PART
Supersedes all previous contracts, letters of intent, memoranda of agreement, letters of interpretation.
WHEREAS it is the intent and purpose of the Company to operate its mining property at Yellowknife in accordance with sound business principals, to minimize the effect of its mining operations on the environment, to promote and foster
Page 5
harmonious relations between the Company and its employees, and to constantly improve the safety and health standards of its operations.
NOW THEREFORE THIS AGREEMENT WITNESSETH that the parties hereto hereby agree as follows:
ARTICLE 1 DEFINITIONS
1.01
The term “employee” or “employees” as used in this Agreement, means all employees of the Company at the Company's property at Yellowknife, Northwest Territories, except managerial supervisory, technical and certain other specific personnel described and excluded by the Order of the Canada Labour Relations Board issued on the 11 th day of April, A.D. 1986.
1.02
A “grievance” is defined as a violation or an alleged violation of a specific article of this Agreement, or of an interpretation of this Agreement.
1.03
“Basic Hourly Rate” is that rate shown for each job classification in Schedule “A” of this Collective Agreement plus applicable Gold Premium wage adjustments, if any, as
Page 6
provided under the terms and conditions of Schedule “B” Gold Scale.
ARTICLE 2
BARGAINING AGENCY AND RECOGNITION
2.01
The Company recognizes the Union as the exclusive bargaining agency for all employees covered by this Agreement for the purpose of conducting collective ^ bargaining regarding rates of pay, hours of work and other working conditions and will continue to do so for the life of this l Agreement so long as the Union retains its right to conduct collective bargaining on behalf of such employees under the law.
2.02
It is not the intention of the Company that employees outside of the Bargaining Unit will do Bargaining Unit work except in the cases listed below. It is recognized however, that staff employees may occasionally become involved in work to a minor degree. (“For example a supervisor may help an employee line up a pipe on a project but is not expected to actually assemble the piping.")
a)	Emergencies for reason of safety or health or to prevent environmental damage or damage to property or equipment.
b)	For the purpose of legitimate instruction.
Page 7
c)	In case of breakdowns after all reasonable efforts have been made to locate and utilize the required type of Bargaining Unit employee.
d)	Experimentation, after notification to the Union in writing:
i)	In different ways of doing a job.
ii)	In ways of operating new or different types of equipment.
2.03(a)
In the event of a violation of Article 2.02 the Company shall pay to the employee, if the grievance is substantiated, an amount equal to the pay had the employee done the job.
2.03(b)
In the event of a violation of 2.02 where no employee has been deprived of an opportunity to work, and the violation is substantiated, the Company shall pay to a charitable or service organization listed below, an amount equal to the pay had an employee of the right classification done the job, with a minimum of four (4) hours pay. The Company shall send a copy of the letter accompanying each payment to the Union. Payment shall be made to the following charitable organizations:
Canadian National Institute for the Blind (1 st quarter of the year)
Page 8
The Abe Miller Workshop (2nd quarter)
NWT Council for Disabled Persons (3rd quarter)
Salvation Army (4th quarter)
2.04(a)
Work of the type being performed by an employee of the Bargaining Unit will continue to be performed by a member of the Bargaining Unit, if such a classified job is still being performed.
In order to ensure that the work is completed on time, the p- Company may contract out the work, but such contracting out JiJ'Will not affect the jobs presently held by Bargaining Unit ) employees. The Company agrees to consult with the Union on contract work in excess of $15,000, but reserves the right to make the final decision having in mind the efficient operations of its business.
For the purpose of this article, displacement shall be defined as having been removed from one's job classification.
If both parties can agree as to what person or persons have been displaced, the Company will ensure that any employee temporarily or permanently displaced by contracting out will not suffer loss of earnings, though he may have to accept other work.
Page 9
2.04(b)
Notwithstanding the grievance and arbitration procedure (Articles 9 and 10), any and all disputes under this article shall forthwith be referred for binding resolution to the contracting out umpire who for the term of this agreement will be Vince Ready, or if he is unavailable, Don Munroe. The contracting out umpire may determine his own procedures case-by-case with the aim of the greatest possible expedition. In appropriate cases, as determined by the contracting out umpire, the procedure may include hearings by telephone conference call and/or by written submissions.
2.04(c)
Where a proposed contracting out is for the purpose of exploration and development of the mine, the contracting out umpire shall be empowered to grant relief from the provisions of this article (on such terms, if any, as the contracting umpire considers appropriate) where in the opinion of the umpire it is reasonable in the circumstances to do so. However, no such relief shall be available to the Company unless the Company has sought such relief in advance of. the proposed contracting out; and in no event will such relief be granted where it appears to the contracting out umpire either that an existing employee will be displaced or laid off as the result of the proposed contracting out, or where it appears to the contracting out umpire that the bargaining unit is being undermined by attrition.
2.04(d)
The Union and the Company shall each pay one-half of the fees and expenses of the contracting out umpire.

ARTICLE 3
NO DISCRIMINATION OR INTIMIDATION
3.01
There shall be no discrimination or intimidation by the Company or the Union against any employee because of membership, non-membership, Union activity, or lack of Union activity, in any lawful union; nor because of race, creed, colour, sex, nationality, religious or political beliefs or personal prejudice.
ARTICLE 4 MANAGEMENT
4.01
The Union recognizes the right of the Company to operate and to manage its business, in all respects, in accordance with its commitments and responsibilities, and to make and alter when necessary the methods, procedures, work rules, and conditions in the interests of efficiency or necessity. Such changes shall not be inconsistent with the provisions of this Agreement. The Company shall always have the right to hire, discipline, promote, demote, lay off and discharge employees who have completed their probationary period for just cause.
4.02
Nothing contained in this Agreement shall be deemed to obligate the Company to continue to operate any of its plants or properties or any part thereof.

ARTICLE 7
SAFETY AND HEALTH
7.01
The Company and the Union recognize the need for the fullest co-operation on all matters pertaining to safety and health on the job.
They agree that each has a responsibility to promote safety and health on the job.
The Company has the responsibility of making available the necessary personal protective equipment for its employees, of ensuring that all operating equipment is properly guarded, that the necessary safety devices are available and in working order, of ensuring that all working places are in a safe condition and that the necessary steps have been taken to ensure that the harmful effects of dusts, fumes, chemicals have been minimized to acceptable levels. The Company will also ensure that its employees are properly trained in the handling of any hazards they might meet.
Page 16
The Union has the responsibility of ensuring to the best of its ability, that its members abide by the safety rules, both Company and Governmental, that apply to their work but will not be held liable for any accident that may occur. It is recognized that the purpose of accident investigation is not to establish blame or liability, but to discover the cause in order to prevent a re-occurrence.
The Union and the Company shall discuss all present and future safety rules whenever either party feels that there is need.
7.02	(i)
There shall be a Mine Occupational Health and Safety Committee comprised of six (6) members from the Bargaining Unit and six (6) members from Management. This committee is responsible for, among other things, seeing that there are four (4) days devoted to inspections underground per month and two (2) days devoted to inspections on surface per month.
This Committee shall act as an advisory body to the Company and shall distribute written copies of accident/incident reports as soon as they are available to the General Manager, Underground Safety and Training Coordinator, Union President and the Chairpersons of the Mine Inspection Services.
7.02	(ii)

The Company will be responsible for selecting its representatives and the Bargaining-Unit shall be responsible for selecting its representatives.
Any employee engaged on this committee working the eight (8), nine (9), ten (10) or twelve (12) hour schedule shall attend such inspections and return to his workplace immediately thereafter.

GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE
9.01	(a)
Stewards, who must be employees of the Company, will be chosen by the Union. The Union will use its best efforts to have Steward representation from each Department. The total number of Stewards will not exceed one (1) Steward for each fifteen (15) Unionized employees. Further the Union may appoint two (2) additional Stewards at its discretion.
9.01(b)
There shall be a Plant Grievance Committee consisting of not more than six (6) Stewards, with representation where possible from each of the Mine, Mill, Mechanical, Electrical, Construction and Pit Departments.
Page 35
9.01(c)
The Union will notify the Company in writing of the names of the Stewards for each department and of the names of the members of the Plant Grievance Committee and of any changes that may occur in the same. The Company shall not be required to recognize the Stewards of the Committee until it has been duly notified in writing by the Union of the persons so selected.
9.01(d) Procedures
Employees may discuss any complaint with their Shift Boss or Foreman, so that it may be quickly resolved if possible. Unless there exists a condition which may endanger employees or equipment, these discussions should take place during the supervisor's visit to the working place, or at the end of the shift. However, should any difference arise between the Company and the Union, or any employee or employees, concerning the interpretation or an alleged violation of this Agreement, an earnest effort shall be made to settle such differences without undue delay in the following manner:
Stage One
The employee or employees concerned may, provided it is done with reasonable promptness, and in no case more than seven (7) calendar days after the alleged grievance has arisen, present his (their) grievance to his (their) immediate Supervisor, and if he (they) desire(s), may be accompanied by a Steward. The immediate Supervisor must present his reply no more than seven (7) calendar days after the grievance has been presented. The employee or employees then has (have)
Page36
seven (7) calendar days after receipt of the Company’s reply to accept the decision or proceed to Stage Two.
Stage Two
The employee or employees concerned, accompanied by his (their) Steward may take up the matter with his (their) Department Head, presenting the grievance in writing. The Department Head or his designate must schedule, within seven (7) days, a mutually agreeable time and date, for a meeting. The Department Head may have a representative from Human Resources or the grievor’s supervisor along with other mutually agreeable management or Union Representatives present at the meeting.
The Company must give its decision no more than seven (7) calendar days after the Second Stage meeting. The Union then has seven (7) calendar days after receipt of the Company’s reply to accept the decision or proceed to Stage Three.
Stage Three
The grievor or grievors together with the Plant Grievance committee, who must have the authority to drop grievances, and if the Union desires a local, district or national representative of the Union, may take up the matter with the Manager or his designate, who must have the authority to grant grievances, with such supervisory personnel immediately involved. Other representatives of the Company may be present if the Company so desires. The complete written record of the grievance, including the decision at Stage Two, must be presented at that time. The Company has fifteen (15) calendar days after the Third Stage meeting to give its reply in
Page37
writing. It is agreed that the parties will schedule at least one (1) meeting per month to hear outstanding grievances at Third Stage.
The Union then has thirty-five (35) calendar days after receipt of the Company’s reply to accept the decision or advise the Company that they are proceeding to arbitration.
9.01(e)
Any grievance arising out of a discharge will commence at the Third Stage of the grievance procedure. The grievance must be submitted to the Mine Manager or designate no more than seven (7) calendar days after the discharge occurred and the Mine Manager or designate will schedule a special Third Stage meeting within seven (7) calendar days of receipt of the grievance.
9.02(a) General
Any difference arising directly between the Union and the Company concerning the interpretation or an alleged violation of this Agreement may be submitted by either party in writing to the other at Stage Three, with reasonable promptness.

Page 42
10.03
The arbitrator shall conduct the hearing in the most expeditious manner possible. To that end:
(i)	The parties shall make every effort to provide the arbitrator with a statement of facts which are not in dispute. The parties will also provide the arbitrator with the grievance presented at Stage Two, the decision of the Company at Stage Three, or, in the case of a difference between the Union and the Company, the written application by the applicant for arbitration stating the difference and the reply thereto by the other party.
(ii)	The parties shall make comprehensive opening statements in which the facts and issues as each party understands them are fully narrated. To the extent possible, the parties will avoid the formal calling of witnesses, but may do so to deal with disputed facts or to clarify a matter.
(iii)	Closing arguments will be concise and to the point, with the use of authorities being limited to those offering direct guidance on the issue (s) in dispute.
10.04
In addition to the foregoing, the arbitrator is empowered to make such directions as the arbitrator considers appropriate to ensure the greatest possible expedition, provided always that the parties are afforded a fair hearing. As examples, the arbitrator may direct in appropriate cases that all or part of a
Page43
hearing be conducted by telephone conference call and/or by written submissions.
10.05
The arbitrator's award shall be published to the parties as promptly as possible, and shall state the arbitrator’s conclusions and reasons as briefly as possible. The arbitrator’s award is final and binding on the Union, its members, the employee(s) involved and the Company.
10.06
It shall be the function of the arbitrator to make a decision based on the terms of the Agreement and Appendices. The arbitrator shall not have the power to add to, subtract from, disregard, alter or modify any of the terms of the Agreement and Appendices.
In the event that the arbitrator decides that he has no jurisdiction to rule on a matter referred to him, the case will be dismissed.
10.07
The arbitrator shall, in his decision, specify whether or not the decision is retroactive and the effective date thereof, but in no case shall the decision be made retroactive to a time prior to the date of the Agreement. There shall be no award to either party as to the costs of the arbitration; the Union and the Company shall each pay one-half of the fees and expenses of the arbitrator.
Page44
10.08
If a claim is made for back compensation in cases where reinstatement is allowed, it shall include wages, overtime and any premiums that the person would otherwise have earned, less any compensation that the person might have received from any source during the period claimed. All claims for compensation in other cases, shall include any wages, overtime and any premiums that the person would otherwise have earned.
10.09
Not withstanding any of the foregoing, the arbitrator may assist the parties in mediating a resolution of a grievance, and may make suggestions to the parties in that regard. Where mediation fails, or is not appropriate, an award shall be rendered as contemplated by the foregoing.
