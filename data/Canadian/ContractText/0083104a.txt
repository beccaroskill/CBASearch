ARTICLE 1
DECLARATION OF THE PARTIES
This contract is established in order to:
1.00	A) Promote orderly and harmonious relations between, the Company,
the Union and employees covered by this contract.
B)	Establish wages, hours and conditions of work, and to promote the security and the well being of employees.
C)	Settle amicably and promptly all complaints and grievances that could arise between the Company, the Union and employees during the life of this present contract.
D)	Promote the efficiency of operations.
1.01	RIGHTS OF MANAGEMENT
The Union recognizes the Company's right 1b administer the factory and the industrial enterprise in which it is engaged. Notwithstanding the generality of the foregoing and subject to other dispositions of this contract and including recourse under the grievance procedure as it applies to all matters pertaining to this collective labour agreement, this includes the following:
1)	To maintain order and discipline.
2)	To hire, classify, transfer, promote, demote, lay-off and fire, suspend or reprimand employees forjust reason.

3)	To introduceor change mechanical procedures, study workloads and institutechanges in workloads, in job assignments, in methods of operation,decide methods of remuneration, determine products to be manufactured, production schedules, types and locations of machines and tools used, procedures of manufacturing, control of materials to be used in the products manufactured and increase or decrease operations;
4)	To ensure the productivity and the security of operations, organize and schedule work, direct the work force, which right includes the right to determine the personnel who will be assigned to specific jobs.
1.02	UNION RECOGNITION
The Company agrees to recognize the Union as sole bargaining agent duly authorized to represent, for the purposes of the labour laws, all employees included in the negotiating unit as described in the union accreditation certificateissued by the Ministry of Labour and Employment and of Income Security dated June 8, 1989.
1.03	WORK CARRIED OUT BY STAFF
The tasks covered by the present collective labour agreement and appearing in Annex " A cannot be normally carried out by staff members when an employee is able and availableto do the work. This does not prevent staff members from doing work of an emergency nature or to teach or train employees, to start up new machines or newly repaired machines, to do experimental work, to do development or other work judged by the Company to control quality.
1.04	LEADHANDS
The leadhands are not to be considered as foreman, they cannot disciplineor fire employees. Not withstanding this, if the foreman is not available the instructions of the leadhands must be followed to insure the efficiency of the department.
-2-
1.05	A) WORKDAY
For the purpose of this collective agreement "work day" signifies a day of the week, with the exception of Saturdays and Sundays or Holidays listed in article 12, and days when a department is closed or when an employee is not required to present himself at work.
B)	DAY
For the purpose of this agreement, the word "day" means calendarday.
C)	SPOUSE
For the purpose of this collective agreement, the expression "spouse" means either of a man and a woman who:
1)	are married and cohabitating;
2)	are living together as husband and wife and are the father and mother of the same child;
3)	have been living together as husband and wife for one year or more.
3)	For the purpose of this collective agreement, the masculine pronoun includes the feminine one and vice versa.
1.06	WORK PERFORMED BY STUDENTS
A)	The Company may employ one or more students to do work that has previously been offered to employees covered in the negotiating unit in the same department by seniority during the summer vacation period on regular time. During this time the Company can use one or more students to do the work on overtime in accordance with the procedure established in article 9,
It is understood that students will receive the minimum wage or a higher wage if specified.
B)	A student does not acquire any rights under this collective agreement other than as established by law.
-3-
1.07	Employees normally receive their work assignments and their orders
from their foreman; nevertheless, any other foreman or officer can, at all times deal severely with an employeewho is about to damage equipment, machinery or any other property of the Company.
Each employee must obtain permission from his foreman or his replacement before leaving his department and must also obtain the permission of the foreman of the department where he is going.

If an employee contravenes this article he will have violated this contract and may be fired by the Company.

3.00	WITHHOLDING AND UNION SECURITY
For the duration of this contract, the Company will accept any authorization signed by an employee on an authorization form supplied by the Union for deduction and remittance of his union dues. The Company undertakes to forward monthly the amounts thus collected.
3.01	REVOCATION PERIOD
An employee may cancel or revoke such authorization only between the ninetieth and sixtieth day preceding the expiration date of the present contract or of any renewal of the same. Such revocation will be made by giving notice to the Company. The revocation will not take effect until the date of the expiration of this contract.
Every authorization given by virtue of the present article has to be clean, legible and signed in duplicate by the employee. The Company will keep one copy and will send the other to the employee concerned,
3.02	NOTICE PERT AININGTO UNION DUES
The Union undertakes to inform the Company of the amount of union dues in force at the time of the signature of the present contract and to advise at least seven working days before the date of the deduction of any modification which has to be made of the above dues during the duration of the contract.
3.03	COLLECTION
The Company will collect union dues weekly but will forward to the treasurer of the Union monthly the amounts deducted.

The employer agrees that as a condition of employment all employees have to be and remain members in good standing of the Union. All new employees have to become members after one complete week of work from Monday to Friday.
3.06	INTRODUCTION OF A NEW EMPLOYEE
Following the hiring of a new employee, the foreman will introduce him to the department steward after one week. A copy of the collective agreement will be given to him after his probationary period.

4.00	COMPOSITION
A)	The parties agree to form at the time of the signature of the present contract, a grievance committee composed of six (6) members of which three (3) will be designated by the Company and three (3) others by the Union, but not more than one (1) per department.
Each of these members must have completed a minimum of six (6) months of continuous service to the Company.
Each of the parties agrees to communicate in writing to the other party the names of its representatives on the grievance committee.
B)	If the Company or the Union ask for a meeting of the grievance committee, the members of the committee will be paid for the time lost if they would have otherwise been at work.
4.01	LOCAL (OUTSIDEIREPRESENTATIVE
It is agreed that if the Union or the Company have retained the services of an outside representative, this person can be one of the representatives of the Union or the Company.
4.02	FUNCTIONS
The grievance committee's functions are to help the parties in the study and the settlement of grievances. It may also start a study and discussion on a subject of common interest submitted to its attention by one or the other of the parties.
-7-
4.03	MEETING
The grievance committee will meet if one or the other of the parties so request. Every meeting will take place within a maximum delay of ten (10) working days of the written request of one or the other of the patties.
4.04	AGENDA
Within three (3) working days preceding each meeting, each of the parties to the present contract must submit in writing to the other party the agenda of questions which it wants to discuss at such a meeting. Nevertheless, every grievance referred to the committee before the start of the meeting in conformity with dispositions of paragraph 5.04 or 6.05 may be part of the agenda of the grievance committee.
4.05	MINUTES OE THE MEETING
At each meeting of the Company and the grievance committee, minutes will be taken and signed copies will be exchanged by the parties at the end of each meeting. These minutes will only reflect the discussions between the parties and can in no case be interpreted as an agreement binding on the patties. Every agreement arrived at between the parties has to be clearly indicated on a document countersigned by the parties.
4.06	Every employee whose grievance has been referred to the grievance committee may explain his case to the grievance committee, if the employee and the Union or the employer asks for it. In such a case the employee must leave the meeting after having explained his case. In the case of a collective grievance, one of the employees who has signed the grievance may explain their case to the grievance committee, if the employee and the Union or the employer asks for it. In such a case the employee has to leave the meeting after having explained their case.

ARTICLE 5
GRIEVANCE AND ARBITRATION PROCEDURE
5.00	PREAMBLE
For the purposes of settling all complaints or grievances which an employee may submit, except in cases of grievances arising from the application of article 7 of the present collective agreement which have to be settled according to the procedure specified in article 7,03, the parties establish the following procedures:
5.01	COMPLAINT
When an employee or a steward or a group of employees have a complaint to make, they will submit it verbally to the foreman of the department or to the foreman of the shift and a verbal reply will be given to them. A complaint does not constitute a grievance.
11
5.02	GRIEVANCE FIRST STEP
An employee who wants to make a grievance, will submit it in writing to the foreman of the department concerned or the foreman of the shift personally in the eleven (11) working days following the occurrence, except if the employee is not physically capable to sign such a grievance (for reasons such as sickness or other reason). In such a case his steward may sign in his place and has to submit his grievance within eleven (1 1) working days after the occurrence, or if he was absent from work, then the knowledge as soon as he returns, of the circumstances which gave rise to the grievance. In the latter case, the delay may not exceed six (6) months from the occurrence of the circumstances which gave rise to the grievance.
The foreman of the department or shift must, within five (5) working days after receiving the written communication of the grievance, communicate his written decision to the one who submitted the grievance.
5.03	SECOND STEP
A)	If the foreman of the department or of the shift does not give a decision within four (4) working days, or if the employee is not satisfied with the decision, the employee or the Union can then submit the grievance in writing, signed by one of the members of the grievance committee, to the Personnel Director within four (4) working days after the date of the communication of the written decision of the foreman of the department or the shift, as the case may be, from the expiration of delays specified for this decision.
B)	The Pers onnel Director has to communicate, within five (5) working days after receipt of the written submission of the grievance, his written decision concerning the grievance which had been presented to one of the members of the grievance committee.
■12-
5.04	GRIEVANCE-THIRD STEP
If the Personnel Director did not give the decision within five working days, or if the employee or the Union are not satisfied by the decision, the employee or the Union may refer the grievance, signed by one of the members of the grievance committee, in writing to the grievance committeewithin five working days following the written communication to the Personnel Director or, as the casemay be, from the expiration of the delays specified for this decision. The committee has to meet within five working days from the written notice, The Company has to give a decision in writing within ten working days after this meeting.
5.05	COLLECTIVE GRIEVANCE
A)	A collective grievance, involving more than one department, may be submitted by the Union directly to the second step of the grievance procedure, by submitting it to the Personnel Director within eleven (11) working days as specified in paragraph 5.02, provided that the grievance is signed by at least one of the employees involved and by the steward of the department concerned.
C)	A collective grievance may be submitted by the Union directly to the second step of the grievance procedure by submitting it to the Personnel Director within eleven (11) working days as specified in paragraph 5.02. provided that the grievance is signed by an officer of the Union.
5.06	PRESCRIPTION
A)	If a grievance is not submitted in the delays specified in one or the other steps by the employee and/or by the steward of the department as an intermediary or by the Union, the grievance will be considered as having been withdrawn.
B)	If the written reply of the Company is not submitted in the delays specified in one or the other of the previous steps either the employee or the Union (as the case may be) may submit the grievance in writing to the following steps within the delay specified for it.
13-
C)	If the Company makes a grievance against the Union and the written reply of the Union is not submitted within the delays specified in the third step, the Company may submit the written grievance in writing to arbitration.
D)	All written decisions which have been arrived at between the Personnel Manager of the Company and the grievance committee of the Union will be final and will bind the Company, the Union and the employee or the employees concerned.
ARBITRATION
5.07	DELAYS IN THE CALL FOR ARBITRATION
If a grievance, (other than one arising from the application of article 7 of this contract), is submitted to the grievance committee, in accordance with paragraph 5,04 or 5.05 and if it is not settled in a satisfactory manner by the above committee, the Union will send to the Company by registered letter a written notice for a request for arbitration, as specified in article 5.08.
This notice has to be made within ten working days from the date of the written communication by the Company of its decision.
If within the above mentioned ten working days the Union fails to give notice to the Company for a request for arbitration, the grievance will be considered to have been withdrawn.
Grievances arising from the application of article 7.01 will be settled in the manner specified in article 7.01 B).
14-
5.08	SINGLE ARBITRATOR
A)	When in accordance with paragraph 5.07 above, die Company will have received a notice for a request for arbitration, the grievance will be considered to have been submitted to a single arbitrator to be chosen in rotation among the following arbitrators:
Mr, Marc Gravel Mr, Jean-Guy Clement Mr, Nicolas Cliche Ms, Diane Sabourin
B)	In the case where a single arbitrator cannot act within a reasonable delay (six months) or in the case where the designated single arbitrator has still not rendered his decision in a previous case, the next arbitrator will be chosen.
C)	In the case of the unavailability of the above arbitrators within a reasonable delay, one or the other of the parties may ask the Minister of Labour of the Province of Quebec to designate an arbitrator.
D)	The fees and expenses of the single arbitrator will be paid half by the Company and half by the Union.
E)	By ritual understanding, the parties may modify and/or add arbitrators to the above list.
5.09	DECISION
The single arbitrator referred to in paragraph 5.08 has to render a written decision within thirty days of the hearing of the grievance or the submission of written notes, as the case may be. The decision of the single arbitrator will be final and will bind the parties to the present contract and all the other employees concerned.
15-
5.10	LIMITATION OF POWER
The single arbitrator will not have the power to amend, alter or modify the terms of this contract.
In the case of grievances relating to disciplinary measures, the single arbitrator will have the right to maintain, modify or cancel the decision of the Company. In the case where a single arbitrator does not maintain the decision of the Company he will have the right to order an indemnity not exceeding the loss of salary of the employee in the mill or any lesser indemnity he will find suitable.
5.11	Any claim resultingfrom a settlement of the grievance presented by an employee or a group of employees, following any one of the steps of the grievance procedures or of arbitration, cannot be made retroactive for a period longer than eleven (11) working days preceding the date when the grievance was submitted.
5.12	Re the decision of the single arbitrator pertaining to the payment of a monetary sum: If following a decision of a single arbitrator pertaining to the payment of money, the amount of money is contested, the amount of the monetary indemnity will be fixed by the single arbitrator who has heard the grievance in the first place.
5.13	Each and every one of the time limits specified in this collective agreement may at all times be changed by written agreement between the Company and Union.
DISCIPLINE
6.00	DISCIPLINARY MEASURES
A) In order to maintain discipline and good order, the Company and the Union agree to adopt the following methods and procedures and agree that these will be applied in as uniform, impartial and equitable fashion as possible. The parties agree also that the disciplinary measures will be applied according to the gravity or the frequency of the infractions and may consist of one or more of the following disciplinary measures: verbal or written reprimand, suspension or firing.
-16-
B)	Preceding the first written warning, a verbal warning may be given by the foreman in the presence or not in the presence of the shop steward. Although the Company encourages its supervisory staff to give such verbal warnings before a written warning, it will not be necessary to do so in every case.
C)	It is understood that all violations of the collective agreement and all infractions pertaining to discipline in the mill may become reason for a disciplinary measure.
6.01	FIRST OFFENCE
A written warning by the Personnel Manager, a supervisor, a foreman of the department or the foreman of the shift when the situation demands it: the Company will advise each employee of each written warning which has been placed in the employee's file. Every reprimanded employee, if his written warning is placed in his file, may submit his case in accordance with the grievance procedure.
6.02	SECOND OFFENCE
Suspension without pay for one to three days in the case of a second offence, provided that the second offence took place within twelve (12) months from the date of the first offence, otherwise for the purposes of this clause, such second offence will be considered as being a first offence.
Every month during which an employee has not worked will be added to the twelve (12)months mentioned in the previous paragraph.
17-
6.03 THIRD OFFF.NCF.
An additional disciplinary measure for the third offenceup to and including firing from the Company, provided that such a third offence took place within twelve (12) months from the date of the second offence, otherwise for the purposes of this article, such third offence will be considered to be a second offence.
Every month during which an employee has not worked, will be added to the twelve months mentioned in the previous paragraph.
6.04 ST JMMARY PROCEEDINGS
In the case of a serious offence at work, the Company may dispense with the above procedures and impose disciplinary measures starting from a warning up to firing the employee. The firing by any member of the staff of the Company having a rank below that of the Factory Manager or the Personnel Manager will be subject to revision by the Factory Manager or the Personnel Manager.
6.05	FIRING PERMANENT EMPLOYEES
Every employee fired or suspended for more than one (1)week, who feels that he was unjustly dealt with, may submit his case directly in writing either in person or through the Union to the grievance committeeaccordingto step three of article 5.04, and in such a case steps one and two of the grievance procedure will be omitted.
6.06	COMPENSATION
If a case of suspension has been submitted to the grievance committee or to arbitration according to the terms of paragraph 6,05 above, is deemed to be unjust, the grievance committee or the single arbitrator will have the right to determine, if such is the case, the amount of compensation up to the amount of the pay that the employee lost.
In the case of a firing which has been submitted to the grievance committeeorto ai arbitrator according to the terms of paragraph6.05 above is deemed to be unjust, the grievance committee or the single arbitrator will have the right to order the rehiring of the fired employee and also determine in such a case, the amount of monetary compensation up to this amount of the pay loss by the employee.

6.07 All disciplinary measures will be removed from the tile of the employee twelve (12) months after it has been placed in the file.
Every month during which an employee did not work will be added to the twelve months mentioned in the previous paragraph.
6.08 The Company will give one of the members of the Union's executive committee one copy of all written disciplinary measures, which have been put into the file of the employee within five (6) working days following a receipt of such a disciplinary measure by the employee. This applies only in the cases of first second and third warnings. Such a warning to an employee has to be given to him within fifteen (15) working days after the knowledge of the circumstances which gave rise to the disciplinary measure. In the cases of disciplinary measures for lateness or absences, the time limit will be twenty (20) days following the date of last lateness or absence.
6.09	When an employee receives a suspension for more than one day, the days of suspension will be consecutive.
6.10 The shop steward concerned, or if there if none available a member of the executive committee, will sign the written warning, but as an acknowledgement of the warning having been received.

ARTICLE 9
PROCEDURES FOR OVERTIME
9.00	It is understood that the procedure for doing overtime, either for
additional hours or to replace an employee who is absent will be the following:
PROCEDURE FOR OVERTIME IN THE DEPARTMENT
A)	An employee who is working on the same machine or task on the shift which is actually in operation.
B)	If this employee is not available, to those employees on the shift which is actually in operation in the same department, on the same occupation, by rotation in,descending order of seniority, as long as the employee to whom the work is offered can accomplish the desired task.
C)	If the employees in question in the preceding paragraph are not available, the work will be offered to the employees of the department and shift in place, by order of seniority who can do the work.
•33-
D)	If the employee in paragraph A), B) and C) refuse or are not available, the overtime will be offered next to students on the occupation of the shift actually in operation, if there are any, as long as they can fulfil the required tasks.
E)	If the overtime required is for a full shift, the Company may offer the first four (4) hours of overtime to the employees of the shift actually in operation, and the second four (4) hours of overtime to the following shift according to the above procedures.
F)	If the employees referred to in the above paragraphs refuse or are not available, the work will be offered to the employee who works on the same machine or task on the following shift.
G)	If this employee is not available, the work will be offered to the
employees of the following shift on the same occupation in rotation
by seniority as long as they can accomplish the task.
H)	If the employees referred to above are not available, this work will be offered to employees of the department on the following shift, in order of seniority as long as they can accomplish the task.
I)	If the employees referred to above are not available, this work will be offered to the department, in order of seniority as long as they can accomplish the task.
J)	If an employee is transferred temporarily according to the dispositions of article 15.12 A), his regular occupation will be considered as his regular occupation for the purposes of distribution of overtime according to article 9.
9.01	A) If an employee believes that overtime has not been offered to him according to 1he above mentioned procedures, he will so notify his foreman. If a new opportunity for overtime is not offered to him within fifteen (15) calendar days, the employee can then lodge a grievance for payment of overtime lost, and in such a case the delay to claim lost wages specified in article 5.11 will be extended to twenty six (26) calendar days.
B) If an employee refuses overtime which has been offered to him, it will be the responsibility of the employee to notify his foreman when he is ready to do overtime next time.

ARTICLE 10.00 OVERTIME PROCEDURES
10-00 A) OVERTIME DURING THE WEEK
All work carried out during the week in excess of the regular daily hours specified in article 8, will be remunerated at one and one times the regular rate of pay.
B)	OVERTIMEON SATURDAY
All work carried out on Saturday will be remunerated at a rate of pay one and a half times the regular rate of pay. This clause does not apply to a weekend shift as outlined in article 21.
C)	OVERTIME ON SUNDAYS
All work carried out on Sunday will be remunerated at twice the regular rate of pay. This article does not apply to a weekend shift specified in article 21,
D)	OVERTIME QN HOLIDAYS
All work carried out on paid holidays, outlined in article 12 (except Christmas or on New Year's Day), will be remunerated at one and one half times the regular rate of pay.
Any employee who works on Christmas Day and/or New Year's Day will be remunerated at a rate of pay twice that of the regular rate of pay.
•35-
E)	OVERTIME - METHOD OF CALCULATION
An employee may not receive overtime twice for hours worked during a day or a week of work. Overtime will be paid only for those hours which have actually been worked in excess of a regular day or a regular week of work.
It is understood that in the application of this collective agreement, an employee may not receive more than one overtime bonus at any time for the same period of work.
F)	Calculation of overtime will not include shift premiums.
10.01 For the purpose of calculating overtime, one has to take into account the hours which would normally have been worked either on one of the holidays in article 12 and 14 of the present collective agreement or on one of the days when an employee is required to serve on jury duty.

15.00	DEFINITION
For the purpose of this agreement, seniority signifies the total service
accumulated by an employee conforming to the following conditions:
A)	The following rules of seniority apply exclusively within the mill.
For the purposes of applying the rules of seniority, departments will be established in the mill.
B)	TRIAL PERIOD
In order to acquire seniority rights within the meaning of paragraph 15.13, an employee has to have first of all completed a trial period of sixty (60) working days within the negotiating period.
Once his trial period is completed he has acquired his right of seniority and the date of his seniority is established retroactively to the date when he was hired. No grievance may be made concerning the firing, transfer, demotion or lay-off of an employee within his trial period. Nevertheless, unless there are contrary stipulations, such an employee will enjoy other rights and privileges specified in the present agreement. Moreover, it is understood that a probationary employee who works in several departments during his probationary period will have his seniority credited to him in the last department where he completes his probation of sixty (60) days. Nevertheless, this does not apply in the case cf a temporary transfer which has been made at the request of the Company.

15.01 LOSS OF SENIORITY
An employee loses his seniority and his job when he:
1)	voluntarily quits working for the Company.
2)	is fired forjust cause and if this firing is not reversed or modified by the grievance procedure.
3)	fails to report for work after a lay-off within seven (7) days after a notice has been sent to him by registered mail from the Company. If the employee notifies the Company within forty eight (48) hours after the receipt of a notice to return to work that he wants to return to work but is incapable of doing so at the date and the hour specified for reasons beyond his control, his name will be left on the seniority list on condition that the reasons which he gives prove to be true after verification.
4)	if an employee is absent without permission and without a valid reason for five (5) consecutive working days.
5)	is absent because of a lay-off, sickness or accident not covered by the C.S.S.T., in accordance with the following table:
Less than one year: after an absence equal to his seniority at the time of such an absence.
From one year to less than five years: after eighteen months of absence.
Five years and more: after twenty four months of absence.

19.01	WORK RELATED ACCIDENT
A)	It is understood that in the case of a work related accident, which is compensatable within the meaning of the Workman's Compensation Law, the employee who has suffered an accident will receive his regular rate of pay for the time lost on the day of the accident.
B)	The Company agrees to transport at its costs and in the most convenient and safe manner any employee who has suffered a work related accident and who requires care outside the mill.
19.02	The Company and the Union agree to form a safety and health committee to discuss all questions relating to the safety and health of employees at work. The committee is composed of two (2) members nominated by the Union and Three (3) members nominated by the Company. This committee will meet every month during the hours of work, without loss of pay. Minutes of meetings of this committee will be kept in order to control the efficiency of this committee.
19.03	The Company will furnish means of protection required by the law in order to protect its employees against being wounded.
-62-
19.04	Every time that an employee feels that the orders or the instructions received are not correct or do not conform to the collective agreement, he must nevertheless carry out these orders or instructions unless these orders put his life or health in danger.
19.05	The Company has the right to send home any employee who shows up to work or works in an intoxicated condition or under the influence of drugs or if he seems to present a danger to his own security or to that of other employees or to the property of the employees or of the Company.
19.06	A) The Company will furnish annually one pair of security boots to each
employee that either the Company or the law judges necessary, or fifty ($50) dollars annually for the purchase of these security boots.

ARTICLE 21
TIME SCHEDULE OF WEEKEND SHIFTS
21.00	All contrary dispositions specified elsewhere in this collective agreement or annexes are subordinated to what follows below:
REQUIREMENTS AND INFORMATION
In order to meet requirements of production and to respond more fully to the requirements of the market place, the Company, when itjudges it to be necessary, may introduce by occupation. by part of a department, by department or for the whole mill the present time schedule in accordance with the following dispositions.
It is understood that in such a case the Company will inform the Union in
advance.
Before instituting the present agreement it is also agreed that the Company will make sure that the employees work their normal work week in the department or in the departments concerned as specified in the present collective agreement.
Except for reasons beyond the control of the Company, it is understood that every employee who works during the week on the same machines will not suffer any loss of salary during the week because of the two (2) preceding paragraphs.
It is understood that the previous dispositions do not apply to employees who ordinarily work during the week who have been laid off following transfers caused by the stoppage of a weekend shift.
21.01	Employees who have been designated to work on twelve (12) hour weekend shifts will have the right to two (2) periods of twenty (20) minutes each for meals in a place designated for this purpose at a time as close as possible to the middle of their shift. It is understood that their will be no interruption of production.
Employees who have been designated to work on twelve (12) hour weekend shifts will have the right to two (2) periods of ten (10) minutes of rest each in a place designated for this purpose during the shift. It is understood that there will be no interruption of production.
21.02	WAGES AND OVERTIME PREMIUM
Employees working on the weekend shift schedule will receive a premium of 50 % of his pay specified by the collective agreement, for hours of work carried out on Saturday and Sunday.
If an employee on the weekend shift works overtime during the week, he will receive a premium of 50% of his pay specified by the collective agreement for all hours thus worked.
21.03	JOB OPENINGS
The Company will fill jobs according to the dispositions of the collective agreement when the weekend shift is being formed or when there are any subsequent job openings, in the following order:
1)	Employees of the same department who can do the work without training.
2)	Laid off employees from the same department who can do the work without training.
3)	Laid off workers from other departments who can do the work without training.
-65-
4) If the Company has to engage someone for the job, it will train employees who have been laid off first of all from the same department and afterwards employees from other departments who have been laid off.
5)	People from the outside.
21.05	SYMPATHY PAY
It is understood that employees working on weekend shifts will receive a wage equal to the wages lost if they have to absent themselves for reasons of death in the immediate family.
21.06	HOLIDAYS
If a holiday falls on a weekend and if an employee works, he will receive double his pay of the weekend shift provided he works a complete weekend shift. If a holiday falls during the week he will receive an additional amount of eight (8) hours, provided he works two (2) weekend shifts before and after the holiday.
21.07	PROBATIONARY PERIOD
For a weekend shift the probationary shift period will be forty (40) working days.
21.08	WORKING DAYS
For the purposes of this agreement, the expression "working days" signifies a day of the week with the exception of Mondays, Tuesdays, Wednesdays, Thursdays and Fridays, with the exception of the statutory holidays mentioned in article 16 and the days during which a department is closed when an employee is not required to report for work.
21.09	DELAYS - GRIEVANCE - ARBITRATION - TRAINING
A) For the purposes of applying the delays in the grievance and arbitration procedures, specified in article 5, working days will be those specified in 1.05 A) by adding two (2) days to the first step.
•66-
B) The training periods specified in article 15.08 E) will be amended as follows:
5 working days amended to two weekends.
30 working days amended to ten (10) weekends.
21.10	TEMPORARY JOB OPENING
For the purposes of this agreement, and in the application of article 15.07. a temporary job opening will be considered as such, if the Company is informed that an employee will be temporarily absent for two (2) or more consecutive weekends. Nevertheless, the procedure establishedin article 15.03 B) will apply.
21.11	In the event of a curtailment of operations it is understood that the weekend schedule will not be interrupted until a full weekend shift has been completed. Employees thus subject to a layoff may use the rules of seniority to transfer to the five (5) day schedule but only in the same department.
In case of an individual layoff on a weekend shift, the rules of seniority as specified in article 15 will apply but only in the same department. Nevertheless, in hie case of employees working under this agreement, when there is a curtailment of personnel for one or more full weekend schedules,the dispositions of article 15.10 will apply immediately at the end of the weekend cycle but only in the same department.
■67-
21.12	FOUR HOURS OF PAY
All employees who have previously not received notice that their services are not required and who report to work at the usual time at his regularjob, may be assigned by the Company to do four (4) hours of work for which he will be paid the equivalentof four (4)hours of his regular pay.
If, nevertheless, no work has been assigned to such an employee, he will be paid a minimum of four (4) hours at his regular rate of pay, except if, upon his arrival at the mill, it is not possible to assign him to his regular work for reasons beyond the control of the Company, such as among others in cases of steam or power failure or fires.
21.13	OVERTIME
It is understood that the parties will discuss how to adapt overtime procedures to the weekend schedule.
21.14	WORK RELATED ACCIDENT
In the framework of this agreement, at the time of a work related accident (first fourteen (14) days), employees working on a weekend shift will be remunerated according to the same formula as employees working a five (5) day shift.
In such a case a full weekend of work is the equivalentto a normal week of work and the formula to calculatethe remuneration for a five (5) day week will apply.
21.15	ANNUAL VACATIONS
Within the framework of this agreement, a week of vacation is equivalent to one weekend of work and the vacations will start on the Saturday preceding the two last full weeks of the month of July.
•68-
21.16	OTHER POINTS
All other points not mentioned in the present agreement will be discussed and resolved between the parties at the time of the introduction of this schedule, keeping in mind any existing laws.

B) While taking into account articles 25.08, 25.09 and 25.12, an
employee has the right to a continuous maternity leave not exceeding eighteen (18) weeks except if the employer, at her request, agrees to a longer period.
■70-
24.01	The notice may be less than three (3) weeks if the medical certificate certifies that the employee has to stop working within a shorter period of time.
24.02	The division of maternity leave before and after delivery is at the discretion of the employee concerned, within the following time limits:
1)	The employee may stop working at any time starting from the sixteenth (16th) week before the probable delivery date. Nevertheless,starting from the sixth (6th) week before the above date, the Company may demand from a pregnant employee who is still working, a medical certificate certifying that she is unable to work; if the employee does not furnish such a certificate to the Company within eight (8) days, the Company may require her to take advantage of her maternity leave at once by sending her a written notice.
2)	The date on which she has to return to work depends on the date on which the employee left work. The duration of the leave is eighteen (18) weeks. If the employee wants to come back to work before the above time period, she has to produce a medical certificate showing
that she may do so.
3)	If the delivery took place after the expected date, the employee has the automatic right to an extension cf her maternity leave equivalent to the period by which she is late. maternity leave an employee continues to accumulate her seniority and can benefit from the dispositionsof group insurance, medication and hospitalization.

24.12	BIRTH OR ADOPTION
An employee may be absent from work for five (5) days at the birth of his child or the adoption of his child. The first two (2) days of absence shall be remunerated if the employee is credited with sixty (60) days of interrupted service.
This leave may be divided into days at the request of the employee. It may not be taken more than fifteen (15) days after the child arrives at the residence of his father or mother.
The employee must advise his employer as soon as possible.
24.13	PARENTAL LEAVE
The father and the mother of a newborn child, and a person who adopts a child who has not reached the age of compulsory school attendance, are entitled to parental leave without pay of not more than thirty four (34) consecutive weeks.
This does not apply to employees that adopt the child of his consort. Parental leave may not begin before the day the child is born or, in the case of adoption, the day the child is entrusted to the employee within the framework of an adoption or the day the employee leaves his work to go to a place outside Quebec in order that the child be entrusted to him.
It shall end not later than one year after the birth or, in the case of adoption, one year after the child was entrusted to the employee.

ARTICLE 25
NOTICE OF TERMINATION OF EMPLOYMENT OR LAY-OFF.
AND WORK CERTIFICATE
25.00	The employer must give written notice to an employee before terminating his contract of employment or laying him off for six (6)
months or more.
The notice shall be of one week if the employee is credited with less than one year of interrupted service, two weeks if he is credited with one year to five years of uninterrupted service, four weeks if he is credited with five years to ten years of uninterrupted service and eight weeks if he is credited with ten years or more of uninterrupted service.
The notice of termination of employment given to an employee during the period when he is laid off is null, except in the case of employment that lasts for not more than six months each year due to influences of the seasons.

2)	whose contract for a fixed term or specific undertaking expires;
3)	who has committed a serious fault;
4)	for whom the end of the contract of employment or the lay-off is a result of a fortuitous event.
