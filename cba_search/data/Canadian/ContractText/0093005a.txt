PREAMBLE
WHEREAS the Manufacturer is engaged in the manufacture of clothing at its Inkster Factory at 1771 Inkster Boulevard and at its McDermot factory located on the 3rd floor at 85 Adelaide Street, in the City of Winnipeg, in the Province of Manitoba (both herein collectively referred to as the "factory"),
AND WHEREAS the Union is composed of persons engaged in various occupations in connection with such manufacturing who are or will be from time to time employed by the Manufacturer in its factory'
AND WHEREAS the Parties hereto are desirous of promoting the principal of collective bargaining, the avoidance of industrial conflicts, and the regulation of relations between the Manufacturer and employees governed by this Agreement for a term and period, t6he whole as more fully hereinafter referred to:

NOW THEREFORE THIS AGREEMENT WITHESSETH, that the Parties hereto agree as follows:

ABTTrT.E 5	- CONTRACTING OUT
5.01	The Manufacturer agrees that work normally
performed by employees within the bargaining unit shall be performed by employees in the bargaining unit, and shall continue to be so performed by employees within the bargaining unit provided that employees qualified to perform the work are available.
The Manufacturer may contract out work where for sound business reasons it is necessary to do so, however it is the desire of the parties that to the extent it is consistent with sound business reasons, any contracting out shall not be done in such a way as to cause the lay-off, replacement, or reduction in regular earnings of any regular employee directly affected in the bargaining unit.
5.03	Where there exists regular employees on lay-off, subject to the sound business reasons referred to in 5.02, those employees on lay-off shall first be offered the opportunity of performing such work referred to herein, provided they have the required skills and ability, and are available when required.
5.04	Nothwithstanding anything herein contained, no homework shall be sent out or permitted.

7 -
7.01	Seniority is defined, for the purposes of this
Agreement, as the length of service of a regular employee in the bargaining unit and shall be applied as provided for in the Agreement.
Any person employed by the Manufacturer shall be considered probationary for the first thirty (30) days worked. The Manufacturer has the option of extending the probationary period another ten (10) days worked.
7.03	An employee shall have no seniority rights during the period of probation. Upon expiration of the probationary period as hereinbefore provided, the employee shall be considered a regular employee and the seniority of such employee shall date back to the date on which the employment commenced.
7.04	The dismissal, lay-off or failure to recall after lay-off of a probationary employee shall not be the subject of a grievance.
An employee's seniority shall be forfeited with no obligation on the Employer to rehire such employee in the event of any on of the following occurrences:
a)	If	the	employee	quits voluntarily, or
b)	If	the	employee	is discharged and not re-
instated through the grievance or arbitration procedure; or
c)	If	the	employee	is absent from work for more
than six (6) months because of	illness unless he provides	a
written doctor's report to the Employer within on (1) month prior to the aforesaid six (6) month period, stating the employee is expected to be able to return to work and resume his full duties within the six (6) month period following such report, and he does so return to work, or
d)	If the employee retires.
ARTICLE R - BRIDGING OF SERVICE
With respect to vacations, any employee's prior service with the Manufacturer shall be bridged upon rehire, provided that the employee has worked a minimum of four (4) years out of the preceding ten (10) years, In addition, all service by any employee within the ten (10) year period from the initial date of hire shall be bridged.
5
8.02	With respect to accumulation of seniority for the
purposes of lay-off and recall, anY employee after three (3) years of continuous service with the Manufacturer bridged, up to the preceding ten (10) years.
ARTTCT.F. 0 - CHECK OFF
The Manufacturer shall, on a monthly basis, deduct from the wages of each regular employee in the bargaining unit, the amount of the regular membership dues payable by a member of the union, and shall remit the amounts deducted to the Union in Winnipeg by the last day of that month.
The Manufacturer shall, at the request of the Union deduct from the wages of an employee who is a member of the Union, initiation fees, and assessments and shall remit the amounts to the Union, in Winnipeg not later than the thirtieth (30th) day of the same month.
The Union agrees that it shall not be the responsibility of the Manufacturer to deduct or collect arrears of dues from any newly hired employee, who was an employee of another manufacturer, provided that any arrears of dues of an employee which occur during the period of employment with the Manufacturer shall be deducted by the Manufacturer.
The Manufacturer agrees that if the Union provides to the Manufacturer signed authorization from any individual, authorizing the deduction of any such arrears which they accumulated while not in the employ of the Manufacturer, the Manufacturer will so deduct them.
9.05	The Union shall notify the Manufacturer in writing of any change in the amounts of regular monthly membership dues, and of any initiation fees, arrears, or assessments at least one (1) month in advance of the end of the pay period in which the deductions are to be made.
9.06	The Union shall indemnify and save harmless the Manufacturer from any and all claims, demands and proceedings that may arise from making any of the deductions aforesaid.

ARTICLE 1 D „	0 STRIKE/NO LOCKOUT
10.01	During the currency of the Agreement:
1)	The Union agrees that it will not declare, cause, direct, authorize, or consent to, or threaten any strike on the part of the employees, snd that there will be no strike of any employees.
2)	No employee or employees in the bargaining unit shall cause, direct, authorize, consent to, or threaten or take part in a strike.

ARTICLE H - DISCRIMINATION
The Manufacturer shall not exercise or permit discrimination of any kind against any employee by reason of any employee's past or present lawful union activities, and the Union shall not exercise or permit discrimination of any kind against the Manufacturer by reason of its past or present lawful activities in relation to the Union.
The manufacturer and the Union further agree that they will not discriminate against any employee of the Manufacturer or member of the Union or applicant for employment because of race, creed, colour, national origin, sen, age, sexual preference, marital status, or disability: making allowance for the pursuance °f a government initialed Employment Equity Program.
The Manufacturer agrees there shall be no differential in pay for equal work between female and male employees.

ARTICLE 13 ~ HOURS OF WORK AND OVERTIME
13.01	Hours of work for the first shift shall be eight (8) hours per day, Monday through Friday starting at 7:00
a.in. and ending at 3:30 p.m. with an unpaid interval of one half (1/2) hour as a meal break.
13.02	Hours of work for the second shift, if designated by the Manufacturer, shall be seven and three-quarters
(7 3/4) hours per day, Monday through Friday, beginning at 3:30 p.m. and ending at 11:45 p.m. with an unpaid interval of one half (1/2) hour as a meal break.
13.03	Hours of work for the third shift, if designated by the Manufacturer, shall be seven and one-half (7 1/2) hours beginning at 11:00 p.m. on Sunday and ending at 7:00 a.m. on Monday and si:: and three-quarters (6 3/4) hours per day Monday through Thursday commencing at 11:45 p.m. and ending at 7:00 a.m.
There shall be a paid rest period of ten (10) minutes during the first half of each shift and ten (10) minutes during the second half of each shift worked.
All hours worked in excess of Standard Hours shall be considered overtime hours and shall be paid at the Overtime Rate.
Any request for overtime work shall be made to the at least four (4) hours in advance, except in the event of an emergency.
The Manufacturer may request an Employee to work overtime and any such Employee shall co-operate with the Manufacturer in working the requested overtime, but no Employee shall be discriminated against in any way for declining to work such overtime.
The available overtime hours, whenever practicable, shall be divided equally among those Employees who regularly work on the operation for which the overtime is required.
All hours worked on a Saturday shall be considered overtime hours and shall be paid for at the rate of time and one half (1 1/2) .
a)	All hours worked on Sunday shall be paid double time (excluding Inkster, 3rd shift, that work on Sunday evening) .
All overtime rates shall be calculated on the basis of the average hourly earnings for the week in which the overtime was worked, exclusive of the overtime hours, and the said average hourly earnings shall include the increases herein provided for.
The following conditions shall be applied in determining overtime hours:
a)	Employees shall not punch in any earlier than fifteen (15) minutes before the beginning of their scheduled shift unless expressly authorized by their immediate supervisor;
b)	IE an Employee punches in any earlier than fifteen (15) minutes before the beginning of the shift as requested
in writing and authorized by the Employee's immediate supervisor, such Employee will be eligible for overtime pay after completing eight (8) hours of work on any such regular work day and the regular hours of work for the shift shall be available to be worked by such Employee;
c)	Employees who are required by the Manufacturer to commence their work day at any time after the scheduled beginning of the shift shall be paid the Overtime Rate for all hours worked after the scheduled end of the shift of that day:
d)	Employees who commence work at any time after the scheduled beginning of the shift without the permission of the Manufacturer or at the request of the Manufacturer shall only qualify for overtime pay on that day after completing eight (8) hours of work.
All Employees called into work shall be supplied with at least four (4) hours of continuous work or paid for at least four (4) hours at their Guaranteed Hourly Rate.
9
A premium of fifteen (15) minutes per day shall be paid to Employees working on the second shift or third shift, subject to the condition that the Employee works the entire shift.
ARTICLE 14 ~ INCENTIVE SYSTEMS AND WAGE RATES
14.01	All employment during the currency of this Agreement shall be performed on an incentive basis based upon standards determined by Management.
The Manufacturer shall set the standard for each operation or group of operations and in determining the standard, each operation or group of operations shall be set to yield the employee an earning opportunity commensurate with the yield at 100% of the minute value.
Particulars of the piecework rates and standards shall be furnished by the Manufacturer to the Union upon request, and the schedule of piecework rates and standards shall be dept at the Manufacturer's office, easily accessible to the Shop Chairperson.
If an employee feels that a piecework rate or standard is inadequate, the employee shall, follow the grievance procedure as set out in Article 22.
14.05	In addition to the rights contained in the
grievance procedures as set out in Article 22, a Union engineer shall have the right to check piecework rates twice a year after consultation with the Manufacturer, as to the time and day of such appointment.
In the event of any dispute on piecework rates or standards, any employee affected by the piecework rate or standard shall continue to work at the existing rate or
standard until the dispute is settled, and in the event of any adjustment of the piecework rate or standard upon the settlement, the adjustment shall be made retroactively to the date of the grievance as between the Manufacturer and the employee.
The Manufacturer agrees that work on duplicates shall be paid for as follows:
a) Duplicates, whenever possible, shall be made on a piecework basis; however, if no piecework rate has been established an estimated rate shall be supplied by the Manufacturer. It is understood by the parties hereto that in those circumstances where allowances have previously been given, this former practice shall continue.
10
b) It is understood that in no event shall the pay for duplicates be less than the employee's guaranteed hourly rate of pay.

ARTICLE 15 - CHANGE OF OPERATION
15.01	When an operator is transferred to an alternate operation, the operator can be paid on a split incentive bonus to enable the operator to reach a piecework level.
15.02	Where the split incentive system cannot be implemented and the request for a transfer is made at a time when the employee has work at her operation piecework average shall be paid.
12
Where the split incentive system cannot be implemented and the employee has no work on her operation, the employee shall be paid her guaranteed hourly rate.
ARTICLE 16 ~ DELAY TIME
All pieceworkers shall be compensated for time lost by reason of waiting for work and/or for machine down time in the following manner:
a) In the event that the waiting time and/or machine down time exceeds a cumulative total in any day of fifteen (15) minutes, the employee affected shall be paid from minute one for the total amount of waiting time and/or machine down time at the employee's guaranteed hourly rate, which shall be in addition to the employee's piecework earnings.
ARTICLE 17. - LAY OFF AND RECALL
In the event of a lay off of employees by the Manufacturer, the employees affected will be determined by the Manufacturer on the basis of departmental seniority and competency, as determined by the manufacturer.
In the event of a lay off of employees, of more than two weeks duration, the employees affected shall be given on week’s notice of lay off or pay in lieu of such notice.
In the event that an employee is laid off, the employee shall be given an approximate date of recall.
The Manufacturer agrees to inform the Shop Chairperson or designated Shop Committee member of all lay offs in excess of one day, and in the event that the lay off is for a period of more than two (2) weeks, the Manufacturer shall discuss the details with a Union Representative as far in advance of the lay off date as is possible.
Employees who have been laid off shall be recalled before any new employees are hired, upon the basis of seniority, provided the employees on lay off are qualified and competent to do the available work, and employees on lay off for a period of more than two (2) weeks, shall be entitled to at least one week's notice of recall in writing.
13
In the event that an employee obtains work elsewhere while on lay-off, the employee shall not forfeit the right of recall.
ARTICLE 18 - DISCHARGE AND TERMINATION
Unless otherwise provided in this Agreement, no regular employee shall be discharged by the Manufacturer except for just cause. Just cause shall include, but not be limited to the following:
a)	Disclosure of confidential employer information, deliberate falsification of any report or records, including applications for employment, personnel records, time cards, gum sheets and piecework tickets: without the prior consent or acquiescence of the employer.
„ b) Theft of unauthorized removal of property from the Employer's premises.
c)	Deliberate tampering, sabotage or destruction of Employer's property.
The Manufacturer shall notify the Union of the discharge of any regular employee.
In the event that an employee has been unjustly discharged, the employee may file a grievance in accordance with the provisions of Article 22, within five (5) working days from the date of discharge.
In the event of the discharge of a regular employee the Arbitrator on any arbitration on the matter shall have the right to sustain the Manufacturer's action, or to reinstate the employee with full, part or no back pay, or to settle the matter in any way deemed just and equitable.
18.05	No employee(s) shall terminate his, her or their
employment except on one (1) week's notice to the Manufacturer.

19.01	a) The following holidays shall be observed, and
subject to the provisions herein, all employees covered by this Agreement shall be paid a regular day’s pay, up to a maximum of eight (8) hours for such holidays, whether or not such holidays fall on a working day, or non-working day of any week, or during any non-working week.

b)	In the event that any one of the foregoing holidays shall fall on a regular work day other than a Monday or a Friday, the date for the observance of such holiday shall be changed, with the consent of a majority of the membership, to either a Monday or Friday of the week in which the said holiday falls.
19.02	No employees shall be compelled to work on any f
the foregoing holidays.
19.03	No employee shall be compelled to work on any
other legal or bona fide religious holiday, nor shall they be paid for them.
19.04	A new employee shall not be entitled to payment for any holiday on which he or she does not work if he or she has not earned wages for part or all of each day for fifteen (15) days during the thirty (30) calendar days immediately preceding the general holiday.
19.05	An eligible employee shall receive holiday pay,
provided that while work is available, such employee works 15 of the 30 calendar days before the holiday and such employee works both the scheduled work day before and the scheduled work day after the holiday, unless such employee is absent for justifiable cause, and such employee has worked within the time period of seven (7) calendar days before or twenty-one (21) calendar days after the holiday. For the purpose of this .Article, "justifiable cause" shall mean any of the following:	"sickness, lay-off, or
vacation".
19.06	Any employee who terminate their employment during the calendar week in which a holiday occurs, without giving one week’s notice, shall not be entitled to payment for such holiday.
15
Subject to the provisions herein, the payment of holiday pay for all hourly time employees shall be up to a maximum of eight (8) hours times their then guaranteed hourly rate, and the payment of holiday pay for all piecework employees shall be up to a maximum of eight (8) times their then average hourly rate.
In the event that any employee is requested to work and performs any work on any of the aforesaid holidays, such employee shall be paid holiday pay at the rate fo time and one-half (1 1/2) of their guaranteed hourly rate, in addition to the regular payment for such holiday.
The payment for holidays which fall on a Saturday or Sunday shall be based on the pay for the day on which the holiday is observed.
ARTICLE 20 - VACATIONS WITH PAY
All vacation pay provided for herein shall be paid directly to each employee by the Manufacturer on or before June 30th each year during the currency of this Agreement.
Employees concerned by this Agreement shall be entitled to an annual vacation and vacation pay in accordance with the following categories.
a)	Employees who, at December 31st. in any year during the currency of this Agreement, have less than one
(1) year of service shall receive vacation pay calculated at the rate of four percent (4%) of their total gross earnings which shall include holiday pay earned during their period of employment with the Manufacturer for the period ending December 31st in any year, and a vacation on a pro rata basis.
b)	Employees who at December 31st in any year during the currency of this Agreement have completed one (1) year or more of service are entitled to two (2) weeks vacation with pay calculated at the rate of four percent (4%) of their total gross earnings with the Manufacturer which shall include holiday pay earned during the year ending December 31st in any year.
c)	Employees who at December 31st in any year during the currency of this Agreement have completed four (4) or more years of service are entitled to three (3) weeks vacation with pay calculated at the rate of six percent (6%) of their total gross earnings with the Manufacturer which shall include holiday pay earned during the year ending December 31st in any year.
16
d)	Employees who at December 31st in any year during the currency of this Agreement have completed twelve (12) years or more of service are entitled to four (4) weeks vacation with pay calculated at the rate of eight percent (8%) of their total gross earnings with the Manufacturer which shall include holiday pay earned during the year ending December 31st in any year.

ARTICLE 22 - GRIEVANCE PROCEDURES.
22.01	A grievance shall be a controversy between the Manufacturer and any regular employee over the interpretation of or adherence to the terms of this agreement.
22,02	The employee along with the Shop Chairperson or a
representative of the Union shall first discuss the complaint with the employee's supervisor. There will be no grievance until the supervisor has had an opportunity to discuss the matter with the employee and the Shop Chairperson or representative of the Union.
If settlement is not reached after the discussion the grievance shall be submitted in writing to the Production Manager by the employee within five (5) days after the incident giving rise to the grievance. The grievance shall contain:
-a description of the problem and sections of the agreement allegedly violated -a statement of remedy sought -the date of submission -the employee's signature.
The Production Manager or her designate will respond in writing to the grievance, within five (5) days, to the union representative.
22.05	If settlement is not reached under step 1 within
five (5) days of the Production Manager's response, the grievance shall be submitted 'to the Manufacturer which will respond in writing, within ten (10) days, to the union representative.
Step 3
22,06	If settlement is not reached under step 2 within
five (5) days after the date of the written response of the Manufacturer it may be referred by either party to a single arbitrator. If the parties fail to agree upon an arbitrator within five (5) working days after one party has served notice on the other party of its intention to refer the matter to an arbitrator, each shall name three (3) names to the other to be considered as an arbitrator.
21
a)	If the parties fail to reach agreement on an arbitrator within five (5) working days after the date of the referral to arbitration, they shall request the Minister of Labour to appoint same.
b)	When the appointment of an arbitrator has been concluded, the arbitrator shall have the power to determine whether the matter before him is arbitrable. Haying made the determination that the arbitrator has jurisdiction; the arbitrator shall hear and determine the differences and shall issue a decision and that decision shall be final and binding upon the parties.
The arbitrator shall only rule on explicit provisions of the agreement. He shall not have jurisdiction to amend or add to any of the provisions of this agreement nor to give any decision inconsistent with the conditions of this agreement.
The expenses of the arbitrator shall be shared equally by the parties.
If the Union or a griever fails to present a grievance to the next step within the prescribed time, the grievance shall be deemed to have been abandoned, unless and extension of the prescribed time limit is agreed upon by the Union and the Manufacturer in writing.
22,10	For the purpose of time frame specified in this
article, the word "days " will refer to working days.
No grievance may proceed to arbitration without the approval of the representative of the Union.
