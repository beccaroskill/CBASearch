ARTICLE 12 - GRIEVANCE PROCEDURE
12.01	A grievance shall be a controversy between the company and any regular employee over the interpretation of or adherance to the terms of this agreement.
12.02	The employee may first discuss the complaint with her supervisor. There will be no grievance until the supervisor has had an opportunity to discuss the matter with the employee.

12.03	If settlement is not reached after the discussion the grievance shall be submitted in writing to the Production Manager by the employee within five (5) days. The grievance shall contain:
a description of the problem and sections of the agreement allegedly violated a statement of remedy sought the date of submission the employee's signature.
The Production Manager or her designate will respond in writing to the grievance, within five (5) days, to the union representative.
Step 2
12.04	If settlement is not reached under step 1 within five (5) days of the Production Manager's response, the grievance shall be submitted to the Human Resources department which will respond in writing, within ten (10) days, to the union representative.
Step 3
12.05	If settlement is not reached under step 2 within five (5)days after the response of the Human Resources department it may be referred by either party to a single arbitrator. If the parties fail to agree upon an arbitrator within five (5) working days after one party has served notice on the other party of its intention to refer the matter to an arbitrator, each shall name three (3) names to the other to be considered as an arbitrator.
If the parties fail to reach agreement on an arbitrator within five (5) working days, they shall request the Minister of Labour to appoint same.
When the appointment of an arbitrator has been concluded, the arbitrator shall have the power to determine whether the matter before him is arbitrable. Having made the determination that the arbitrator has jurisdiction, the arbitrator shall hear and determine the differences and shall issue a decision and that decision shall be final and binding upon the parties.
12.06	The arbitrator shall only rule on explicit provisions of the agreement. He shall not have jurisdiction to amend or add to any of the provisions of this agreement nor to give any decision inconsistent with the conditions of this agreement.

13 - LAY OFF AND RECALL
In the event of a lay off, the employees affected will be determined by department seniority and competency as determined by the Manufacturer.
In the event of a lay-off of more than two (2) weeks duration, the Employees who will be affected, shall be given one week's notice of lay-off or pay in lieu of such notice.
When a lay-off of more than two (2) weeks duration's required, the Manufacturer shall discuss the details with the Union as far in advance of the lay off date as possible.
Employees laid off shall be recalled before any new Employees are hired, provided they are qualified to do the available work. Employees laid off pursuant to the provisions of paragraph 13.02, shall be entitled to at least one week's notice of recall in writing.
In the event that an Employee obtains work elsewhere while on lay-off, he or she shall not forfeit his or her recall rights.
In the event that an Employee is laid off he or she shall be given an approximate date of recall.
The Shop Chairperson or the designated Shop Committee Member shall be informed of all lay-offs, in excess of one day.

14

ARTTCT.E 21 - WAGE RATES
21.01	Employees paid on a piecework basis shall have their remuneration calculated on the minute value rate as set out in Appendix A. However, employees shall not receive remuneration which shall be less than the sum of their guaranteed rate, as set out in Appendix B, multiplied by the number of hours worked.
21.02	The increases in wages shall each be compounded over the term of the agreement.
21.03	Where the increase in wages does not bring an employee up to the Progression Scale, the employee shall receive the difference on the anniversary date of the agreement.
21.04	Where the increase in wages brings the employee to an amount greater than the Progression Scale, the employee shall receive the greater amount and all increases shall be compounded on that amount.
21.05	Employees shall not receive less than the one year progression scale after one year employment.
21.06	Wherever job classifications are used herein, same shall have the meanings outlined in Appendix C.

24.03	If the Manufacturer and Representative of the Union are unable to settle the dispute referred to in paragraph 24.02 herein, within ten (10) days, the matter shall be determined by arbitration in accordance with the provisions of Article 12 hereof.

24.05	Regular Employees who are requested by the Manufacturer to change operations on a temporary basis, whether a piecework or time work basis, shall be paid their average hourly rate of their previous six (6) months’ earnings if at the time of the said request by the Manufacturer his or her regular operation is available.

APPENDIX C
Job Classifications
1. Cutter /l
Check all components in markers to ensure all pieces in cut against the sample, lay up materials including plain, patterned, plaid, striped, shells, interlinings & linings by hand or machine to the number of lays required to cut, exercise judgement in splicing damaged raw materials, block and match plaids. Perform material utilization and instructions (nap, face-up, number of lays, pins, etc.) by memo or written documentation. Cut all materials with knives, shears and electric cutting machines.
2	. Cutter #2
Same duties as Cutter #1 except for plaids, stripes.
3	. Spreader
Spread material including plain, patterned, plaid, striped, shells, interlinings and linings by hand or machine to the number of lays required to be cut, exercise judgement in splicing damaged raw materials, block and match plaids.
4	. Bundlers
Assemble, bundle or separate parts of garments for further operations after the garments or parts including trimmings have been cut, operate a soabar machine, separate piecework tickets into the proper operations and attach same to the applicable bundles.
5.	Fusers
Fuse together different parts of a garment using a special machine, monitor quality and run tests, set the machine to proper specifications.
6.	Operators
Perform any machine sewing operations necessary to complete a garment.
28
7.	Underoressers
Press seams and do other incidental piece pressing necessary to make the pieces ready for the operator or the finishing process.
8	. Final Pressers
Press any garment after it has been sewn.
9	. General Hand
Do sundry factory work incidental to the manufacture of garments and, without limiting the generality of the foregoing, including thread clipping and cleaning, but not including service workers.
