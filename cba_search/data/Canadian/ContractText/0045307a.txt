ARTICLE I - SCOPE
THE COMPANY recognizesthe Union for the duration of this Agreement as the sole bargaining agent for the purpose of collective bargaining in respect to wages and other conditions of employment on behalf of the Company's employees within the following bargaining unit, namely: all employees employed in the Office, Laboratory, Elevator, Steam, Seed Plant and Oil Packaging of CSP Foods' Saskatoon location, except casual employees, all staff of the Corporate Office, and incumbents of the following location positions: Location General Manager, Director Retail Sales, Director Sales and Marketing - Western Canada, Operations Manager, Manager Packing, Warehouse & Bakery Mix Departments, Manager Commodity Processing Development, Manager Grain Procurement and Special Crops, Manager Milling, Transportation Services Manager, Manager Warehouse Services, Project Manager, Territory Sales Manager, Indusfrial/Export Sales Manager, Manager Retail Sales Western Canada, Manager Value-Added Accounting, Manager Marketing Accounting Services, Manager Product Development/Quality Assurance, Baking Centre Manager, Manager Grain Transactions, Controller, Feed Ingredient/lndustrial Sales Representative, Sales Representative (2), and Administrative Co-ordinator.

ARTICLE II - SPIRIT AND INTENTION
The spirit and intention of this Agreement is to maintain good and amicable relations between the Company and all of its employees covered by this Agreement so that the solution of all matters pertaining to conditions of employment shall be arrived at by
4
consultation and agreement between the parties hereto and this Agreement is in no sense to be taken as a discouragement to direct negotiations where a solution can be reached by such means without having recourse to the bargaining procedure hereinafter provided.
ARTICLE HI - RECOGNITION
1.	The Union recognizes that the Company has sole authority to manage its affairs, to direct its working forces, including the right to hire, to fix wages within the terms of Schedule A (Article XXIII) hereinafter referred to, transfer, promote, demote and to suspend or
discharge any employee for just cause, and to increase or decrease the working force of the Company, to reorganize, close, disband any Department or sections thereof from time to time as circumstances and necessity may require, subject to the right of any employee concerned to lodge a grievance in the manner and to the extent hereinafter provided.
2.	The Union further recognizes the right of the Company to operate and manage its business in all respects in accordance with its commitments and responsibilities and to make and alter from time to time the rules and regulations to be observed by the employees, not inconsistent with the terms of this Agreement.
ARTICLE IV - COMPANY RELATIONS
It is understood and agreed, inasmuch as the Company recognizes the Union as an employees' bargaining agency, as evidence of good faith, the Union assumes responsibility for its members in their relations with the Company and will use its best efforts to have the employees' responsibility under the contract carried out in letter and spirit and to have its members deliver a fair day's work as called for by the position involved and the reasonable orders of the Company.
ARTICLE V - MAINTENANCE OF MEMBERSHIP
The Company agrees that as a condition of employment, membership dues or sums in lieu will be deducted from the wages earned by employees in the following categories:
a.	All employees for whom the Union has bargaining authority under this collective agreement, and
b.	All new employees under this collective agreement within thirty (30) days of commencement of employment.
Membership dues or sums in lieu so deducted from salaries shall be paid monthly to the General Secretary of the Union.

ARTICLE VII-ARBITRATION BOARD
The Arbitration Board established under Article VI (Step 4) shall not have the authority to alter or change any of the provisions of the Agreement, or to insert any new provisions, or to give any decision contrary to the terms and provisions of the Agreement, but it is agreed that where disciplinary action is involved the Arbitration Board shall have the power to award a penalty or amend a penalty imposed by the Company.
The decision of the Board or a majority of the arbitrators shall be final and binding upon the parties hereto and upon any employee or employees concerned. If there is no decision by a majority of the Board, then the decision of the Chairman shall be similarly final and binding.
3.	No costs of any arbitration shall be ordered to or against either party, but each party shall be responsible for the expenses andlor fees payable to its representative and for one-half (1/2) the expenses andlor fees payable to the Chairman of the Board.
ARTICLE VIII - GROUP INSURANCE
All eligible employees who have completed ninety (90) days of continuous service with the Company shall, as a condition of employment, contribute to the group insurance plans adopted by the Company and the Union. Such contributions shall be made by payroll deduction in accordance with the terms and conditions of the group life and disability insurance plans outlined in Schedule B.

2.	No employee shall be retained as a permanent employee beyond the first day of the month coincident with, or immediately following his/her sixty-fifth (65th) birth date.

ARTICLE X - SICK LEAVE
In the case of sickness, or disability arising out of sickness, all employees who have completed ninety (90) days or more continuous service with the Company, shall be entitled to benefits in accordance with the following plans:
Sick Leave Plan
1.	Employees entitled to payment of wages during sick leave shall be paid at the regular
rate of pay that would apply if the employee had not been absent on sick leave during the period of time covered by his/her accumulated sick leave credits.
2.	Benefits commence the first day of disability and expire on the thirtieth (30th) day of disability.
3.	Extended sick leave benefits will be provided in the amount of 66 and 2/3rds percent of regular earnings to a maximum earnings ceiling of the Unemployment Insurance Commission program at the time of disability for:
a.	the period of absence due to sickness in excess of a two-week waiting period in any one illness,
or
b.	where sick leave credits exceed two weeks, extended sick leave benefits shall commence at the expiration of sick leave credits.
Extended sick leave benefits payable in any one illness shall cease on the thirtieth (30th) day of disability.
Benefits under this provision are reinstated immediately on return of an employee to work following an illness.
4.	Employees eligible for Workers’ Compensation benefits are not eligible for benefits under this plan.
5.	Sick l eave Credits
a.	Sick leave credits are earned at the rate of 1-1/4 working days per month of continuous full-time employment to a maximum of twenty-two (22) working days.
8
Sick leave credit accumulation during the probationary period will be recognized upon successful completion of the probationary period for sick leave from that time on.
Once an employee has earned twenty-two (22) working days’ sick leave credit, no further credits are required for full coverage under the Short Term Disability Plan.
Sick leave usage is not deducted from accumulated sick leave credits.
Present permanent employees will receive sick leave credits on the basis of time served with the Company.
1.	Sick leave allowance payments shall not extend beyond normal retirement age.
2.	Sick leave allowance payments for the first day of any sickness may be withheld at the discretion of the Company.
3.	All recipients of sickness and disability allowance payments must provide, on request of the Company, medical reports of their condition.
4.	The maximum period of sick leave on which vacation time shall accumulate shall not
exceed two months in any calendar year.
5.	Benefits from the three plans, the sick leave plan, the extended sick leave plan and disability income insurance plan, shall be integrated.

ARTICLE XII - SENIORITY
1.	No employee shall acquire seniority until he/she has completed satisfactorily a probationary period of ninety (90) days, at which time his/her seniority shall be retroactive to the date of hiring.
Seniority shall be calculated, on a cumulative basis, to include all service with the Company including all service with the Saskatchewan Wheat Pool Industrial Division as referred to in this Agreement in Definitions: 2. Service with the Company.
2.	a. In the matter of promotion, demotion, transfer, layoff and recall, seniority shall
prevail subject to qualifications, ability and merit. For the purpose of this clause seniority shall be defined to include employees in the Office, Laboratory, and Plant (i.e. those positions identified in Schedule "A" of this Agreement).
b.	Where it is elected by the Company to fill a vacancy to a position within the scope of this Agreement, the position shall be posted for a minimum of five (5) working days and such postings shall be placed in all departments. In the selection process, seniority on a bargaining unit basis shall prevail subject to qualifications, ability and merit. An applicant having more seniority than the appointed applicant may request reasons in writing for not receiving the appointment.
c.	Employees on an authorized leave of absence, vacation or sick leave shall be considered by the Company as applicants for a vacancy which Management elects to fill.
d.	Notwithstanding b., the Company in its sole discretion may elect to fill a vacancy to a position within the scope of this Agreement by transfer. Employees may submit requests for transfer and the Company shall maintain such requests on a transfer list. The Union may make representation to the Company where the circumstances of the transfer in its view warrants such representation or where the Union feels that the transfer interferes with the rights accorded by this Agreement.
e.	.Wherethe selection process described in (b) does not result in appointment being made, the Company may fill the position by other means.
3.	It shall be the policy of the Company that in filling staff vacancies, present .members of the staff will be given first consideration.
10
4.	An employee who is appointed to an out-of-scope position shall not accumulate seniority but shall retain the seniority he/she earned prior to his/her appointment. Within one year of such appointment, he/she may exercise his/her earned seniority to return to his/her former in-scope position but after one year, he/she may exercise his/her earned seniority only to return to a vacant in-scope position graded not higher than that which he/she occupied prior to his/her out-of-scope appointment.
5.	In the case of staff reduction, employees shall be entitled to exercise seniority rights on a service-with-the-Company basis with respect to transfers to positions of the same or lower grade within the bargaining unit. The exercising of seniority rights under this provision shall be confined to those positions which have been designated by agreement of the Company and the Union, as having qualification requirements which are equal to, or less than, those possessed by the employee wishing to exercise such rights.
6.	Seniority lists shall be updated and posted quarterly.
7.	Seniority for General Duties employees will be calculated on the basis of forty (40) hours = seven (7) calendar days.
8.	Employees, with the exception of probationary employees, shall continue to earn seniority credits while on Workmen’s Compensation.

medical doctor certifying that she is pregnant, that employee is entitled to and shall be granted a leave of absence from employment of six (6) months:
i)	The term of leave shall be for a minimum of seventeen weeks unless the employee provides notice as provided in sub-section (iii) that she wishes it to be a shorter period.-.
ii)	The commencement of the leave shall be at the discretion of the employee.

A)	On the expiration of any leave of absence from employment taken by her under paragraph (a)

C)	On the day the child is born or comes into his/her actual care and custody, and

ARTICl F XIV - PROBATION AND TERMINATION OF EMPI OVMENT
1.	Monthly Salaried Employees
a.	i) On initial appointment or on promotion, all monthly employees shall be

ARTICLE XV- SEVERANCE ALLOWANCE
14
1.	When a classification covered by this Agreement is permanently discontinued due to
automation or reorganizationthen the displaced employee shall be entitled to transfer to another position in accordance with the seniority provisions of this Agreement.
2.	Should an employee accept a demotion to a lesser-paid position under the provisions of Section 1 above, then his/her salary shall be reduced in accordance with the formula in Schedule A. 5(d).
3.	When the Company is unable to provide comparable employment to a displaced employee referred to in Section I above or the employee is unwilling to accept alternate employment involving a demotion, then provided the employee has ninety (90) days or more continuous service with the Company, he/she shall on termination be entitled to a severance allowance prorated on the basis of two (2) weeks pay for each year of service.
ARTIC1 F XVI - TEMPORARY PERFORMANCE OF HIGHER DUTY
1.	An employee assigned to relieve in a higher-paid position shall be paid temporary performance pay at the rate of pay he/she would have received had he/she been promoted to the position.
2.	Management reserves the right to make the determination as to whether temporary performance assignment is actually necessary.
3.	Application of temporary performance of higher duty shall be limited to temporary substitution of sixty (60) days or less, except in cases of compassionate leave or in cases of sickness where medical advice indicates the regular incumbent of the position will be able to return to the position.
4.	When it is know that temporary performance will be for a period of sixty (60) days or more, the position shall be posted as a temporary position.
5.	It is further understood that experience obtained by an employee in temporary performance of higher duty shall not be considered in qualification of an employee for promotion to a position on a regular basis.
6.	When performing out-of-scope duties when assigned by the Department Manager, the employee will receive a 5% premium based on their current regular rate of pay.
ARTICLE XVII - HOURS OF WORK
1.	Office Employees
15
The normal hours of work for office employees shall be confined to a five (5) day week and shall be thirty-five (35) hours.
2.	Laboratory Employees
Effective December 1,1983, the normal hours of work for laboratory employees shall be seven and one-half (7-1/2) hours per day and thirty-seven and one-half (37-1/2) hours per week. Laboratory technicians will be rotated to provide seven (7) day coverage. The normal hours of work shall be between 0700 hours and 1700 hours as determined by the workload. Days of work and hours of work shall be arranged and scheduled by mutual agreement between the Union and the Company to meet the needs cf the plant operation.
3.	Plant Employees
Forty (40) hours shall constitute a basic work week.
a.	Shift Workers - When the Plant is in production on a twenty-four (24) hours-per-day, seven (7) days-per-week basis, employees shall be assigned to rotating shifts according to an agreed shift schedule consisting of eight (8) hour shifts. The starting time of shifts shall be 8:00 a.m., 4:00 p.m., and 12:00 midnight. Shifts will rotate in a clockwise manner. Changes in the shift schedule shall be negotiated and agreed to by the Company and the Union.
Employees shall be given twenty-four (24) hours of notice when shifts within the established schedule are changed. In the event of failure to provide notice of change in shift the employee will be paid one and one-half (1-1/2) times the regular rate for the first shift following the change, except where the change is due to failure of another employee to report for duty as scheduled and with less than twenty-four (24) hours of notice. Premium time paid under this clause shall not duplicate daily or weekly overtime paid for the same hours worked.
b.	Shift Millers - Shall work eight (SJconsecutive hours on their respective shift. The normal work week for this class shall not exceed forty (40) hours.
c.	Other Workers - The work week shall consist cf five (5) consecutive eight (8) hour days. The hours of work each day shall be 8:00 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. with one-half (1/2) hour during this period for lunch.
These work schedules may be changed when necessary due to staff changes, sickness or accident or when necessary to arrange vacations. Changes in the work schedule may be arranged by mutual agreement between the Company and the Union. When a shift workman wishes to change his/her shift for personal reasons, he/she may do so only with the approval of the Department Manager or Production Manager.
16
4.	Elevator Assistants and Helpers
The normal hours of work for Elevator Assistants and helpers will be eight (8) hours per day and forty (40) hours per week averaged over a thirteen (13) week period in accordance with provisions of the Canada Labour Code Regulations.
5.	Actual daily hours of work and time off for meals shall be arranged by the Production
Manager and the Executive of the Union and the actual daily hours may be re-arranged by mutual agreementto meet their mutual convenience, provided that for all employees except those listed in 4. above where averaging provisions under the Canada Labour Code Regulations apply, the normal work day shall not exceed eight (8) hours.
ARTICLE XVIII - OVERTIME
Overtime shall be worked when authorized by the Company. Employees shall co-operate with the Company in performing overtime. However, when the Company determines that it is necessary to work overtime and there are no or insufficient volunteers, the Company has the right to assign employees to work overtime.
1.	Office Employees
a.	Authorized overtime for hours worked in excess of thirty-five (35) hours per week shall be paid for at one and one half (1-1/2) times the normal rate of pay.
b.	All time worked on Saturday will be paid for at the rate of one and one-half (1-1/2) times the normal rate of pay.
c.	A minimum of three (3) hours pay at overtime rate will be paid to an employee who is called out for duty by management after the employee has left the Company premises.
2.	Laboratory Employees
Authorized overtime for hours in excess of seven and one-half (7-1/2) hours per day and thirty-seven and one-half (37-1/2) hours per week shall be paid for at the rate of one and one-half (1-1/2) times the normal rate of pay.
3.	A minimum of three (3) hours’ pay at regular overtime rates shall be paid for "call-outs”
4.	Elant
a.	Authorized overtime on all time worked in excess of regular hours will be paid as
follows:
17
i)	One and one-half (1-1/2) times the regular rate for hours worked in excess of eight (SJhours in any one day;
ii)	One and one-half (1-1/2) times the regular rate for hours worked in excess of forty (40) hours in any calendar week, except where such hours in excess of forty (40) are part of an agreed rotating shift;
iii)	Two (2) times the regular rate for all hours worked in excess of twelve (12) hours in any one day.
b.	Authorized hours of work in excess of the forty (40) hours per week average identified in Article XVII 4., shall be paid for at the rate of one and one-half (1-1/2) times the employee’s regular rate of pay.
c.	A minimum of four (4) hours at regular overtime rates shall be paid for "call-out”.
d.	Overtime on an employee’s scheduled day of rest shall be at the rate of one and one half (1-1/2) times his/her regular rate of pay for the first eight (SJhours and double time for hours worked in excess of eight (8) hours for the first day of overtime and any hours worked on subsequent days of overtime during the rest period.
ARTICLE XIX - SHIFT DIFFERENTIAL - OFFICE
Employees in all classifications, except Caretakers and those working rotating shifts, shall be paid a shift differential of twenty cents (208) per hour for hours worked outside regular normal working hours as established in Article XVII. The differential shall not apply when overtime rates are being paid.
ARTICLEXX -ABSENCE FROM DUTY
No employee shall absent himself from the Employer's premises during the hours of work except with the consent of supervisory personnel.

OR
